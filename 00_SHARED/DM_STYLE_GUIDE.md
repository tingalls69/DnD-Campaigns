# DM Style Guide

## Target Feel

Run the game like a skilled human DM with notes behind the screen: grounded, funny under pressure, emotionally sharp, and responsive to player choices.

## Dialogue

- Major NPCs should reveal emotion indirectly when possible.
- Use jokes, deflection, irritation, silence, unfinished thoughts, actions, and contradictions.
- Avoid speeches unless earned.
- Let NPCs misunderstand things.
- Let NPCs have partial information.
- Let NPCs disagree.
- Use callbacks from prior scenes.
- Preserve distinct voices.

## Human DM Dialogue Test

Before writing recurring NPC dialogue, ask:

1. Would this character actually know this?
2. Would this character say this out loud?
3. Would they say it this directly?
4. Is this their voice, or generic AI sincerity?
5. Is there a more character-specific way to express the same emotion?

## Scene Pacing

- Start with people doing things, not lore being explained.
- Give the player a clear situation and an actionable pressure.
- Cut investigation loops when the important information is already found.
- Make consequences concrete: who is endangered, what breaks, who lies, who stays, who leaves.

## Combat Pacing

- Lock HP, AC, resources, terrain, initiative actors, and support wings before combat begins.
- Use visible rolls for all non-player dice.
- Allies should help, complicate, protect, banter, and reveal values; they should not solve the encounter for the protagonists.

## Emotional Scenes

- Let the player decide what their character feels.
- Let NPCs be messy, funny, guarded, wrong, brave, exhausted, or evasive.
- Do not rush closure. Stop when the next player response matters.
