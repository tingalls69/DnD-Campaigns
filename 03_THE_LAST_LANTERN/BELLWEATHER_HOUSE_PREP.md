# Bellweather House Prep

Use this file when preparing or running the approach to Old Bellweather House / South Lantern Relay. This is DM-facing support, not a script.

## Current Launch Point

The party is in the Greybridge wayhouse after a short rest. The false custody transfer failed. Halven Rusk and the masked coordinator are alive and captured. Pella Morn is safe for now and knows a hidden service route.

Start from player choice:

- interrogate prisoners;
- examine evidence;
- ask Pella about the route or south bell;
- shore up Greybridge security;
- choose whether to move today;
- decide who goes to Bellweather and how.

## Approach Routes

### Main Road

Most obvious and most watched.

Risks:

- false ward marker;
- scouts watching for Greybridge movement;
- official roadblock;
- cleanup-team ambush;
- civilians or local workers used as leverage.

### Hidden Servant-Road

Known from Pella:

- under the rain steps;
- behind the old ash trough;
- accessible when water is running;
- stones move correctly only when water runs.

Strengths:

- avoids the most obvious approach;
- gives Needle, Zula, Mirelle, Corren, and Ves meaningful prep roles;
- lets the party choose stealth, infiltration, or split support.

Risks:

- may be trapped, flooded, watched, or tied to the south bell;
- may require opening or forcing old mechanisms under pressure;
- may require choosing whether to bring Pella close enough to guide details.

### Forced Official Approach

The party uses Greybridge authority, prisoners, evidence, or a fake compliance move to approach Bellweather in the open.

Risks:

- South Lantern may prepare a parley/trap;
- captured evidence may be targeted;
- public action may endanger witnesses left behind.

## Pella Morn Handling

Pella is a person, not a key.

The DM may let Pella provide:

- route description;
- remembered sounds/smells/landmarks;
- warning about the rain steps and ash trough;
- emotional reaction to the south bell;
- small detail that reframes a clue.

The DM should not:

- force Pella to travel;
- make Pella responsible for harm caused by enemies;
- have adults pressure her without giving Brannok/Selene a chance to intervene;
- use her as a lore dispenser;
- trigger the second bell hearing without clear player-facing risk or consequence.

## South Bell Danger Clock

Use only if the party approaches Bellweather, studies bell-linked magic, mishandles the mask/tokens, or brings Pella near a relay effect.

| Clock | Advance When | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 0/4 | starting state | Pella has heard the south bell once in her sleep. |
| 1/4 | relay hum, token pulse, command-mask feedback, or distant bell vibration | Pella feels pressure behind the burn scar; Zula or Selene can notice. |
| 2/4 | bell-linked magic is activated nearby or evidence is mishandled | Pella hears a partial note; she may freeze, dissociate, or remember route details out of order. |
| 3/4 | South Lantern or the relay intentionally targets her | The scar reacts; nearby tokens/beads may answer. |
| 4/4 | second full south bell hearing | Reveal earned consequence in play: marking, command attempt, location ping, memory unlock, or relay activation. Do not make this instant death. |

Ways to reduce or pause the clock:

- Zula countermark / wardwork;
- Selene threshold magic or Dispel Magic;
- physically distancing Pella from the relay;
- muffling or disabling a bell component;
- learning the mask/token mechanism before triggering it;
- player-created protection plan.

## Prisoner Interrogation Leads

### Halven Rusk

Likely knows:

- who gave him the transfer slips;
- whether he is corrupt, coerced, or magically influenced;
- how the South Lantern Continuity Office credential was meant to be used;
- why Pella was singled out;
- what "Paper first. Knife second. Bellweather if exposed" means operationally.

Complications:

- may be gagged for good reason;
- may have coercion involving family or status;
- may try to hide behind official tone;
- may know less than his confidence implies.

### Masked Coordinator

Likely knows:

- attack timing;
- token pairing;
- mask purpose;
- field command structure;
- whether South Lantern can listen or speak through the mask.

Complications:

- mask may control speech, relay commands, suppress identity, punish disobedience, or prevent naming handlers;
- removing the mask casually may injure, silence, alert, or kill the wearer;
- Zula should get a meaningful wardcraft lane before anyone rips it off.

## Evidence To Study

- Hidden South Lantern Continuity Office credential.
- Brass road-arch whistle.
- Three blank transfer slips with real magistrate seals.
- Green-cuff thread fibers.
- Folded Pella note.
- Bound outside road-arch token.
- Broken/suppressed indoor road-arch token.
- Road-arch glass collar beads.
- Hidden forearm blade.
- Magical pale road-arch command mask, alias "Oogabooga," per Ashhelm.

## Bellweather Zones

### Rain Steps / Ash Trough

Entry puzzle/hazard, not a word puzzle. Water matters physically.

Good actions:

- inspect drainage;
- run water;
- move stones;
- brace old masonry;
- send Ves or Needle ahead;
- use Zula's wardcraft;
- use Brannok/Corren strength to hold a shifting route open.

### Servant-Road Tunnel

Narrow, damp, old, practical. Built for workers, not heroes.

Use:

- stealth;
- claustrophobic pressure;
- old route marks;
- a trapped worker or frightened runner;
- first sign of the south bell vibration.

### Burned Wing

Evidence of old structural fire and acceptable-loss history.

Use:

- unstable floors;
- signs people were sealed off;
- rescued victim or hidden witness;
- South Lantern rhetoric about "necessary closure."

### Sealed Road Works Archive

Evidence zone.

Use:

- Mirelle lane;
- Marcella pressure if officials/prisoners are present;
- false route indexes;
- field ledger pages;
- proof that custody destinations were manipulated.

### Ward Relay Chamber

Zula/Selene spotlight zone.

Use:

- road-arch token network;
- command mask resonance;
- relay controls that can save evidence or harm captives if mishandled;
- exact 5-foot tactical distances if combat begins.

### Bell / Signal Chamber

Major danger zone.

Use:

- south bell threat;
- South Lantern contact;
- split priorities: stop bell, rescue people, secure ledger, survive attackers.

## Rescuable People

Bellweather must include living people who can be saved now.

Good options:

- a worker who maintained the rain-step route and knows it was misused;
- a witness hidden in the burned wing;
- a courier who thought they were moving relief papers;
- a guard who realized too late what the transfer system meant;
- children or injured civilians routed toward a false safe point.

Avoid making every victim an exposition machine. One concrete fear and one actionable clue is enough.

## Encounter Scaling

The active party is large. Use support wings.

Spotlight lanes:

- Brannok: hold a route, stop a runner, choose who to protect personally.
- Selene: command Ves, challenge control, threshold/patron pressure.
- Marcella: social tempo, Cutting Words, Bardic Inspiration, counter-pressure.
- Mara: hold door/chokepoint/bell access.
- Needle: scout hidden route, traps, evidence grab.
- Zula: tokens, wards, mask, relay machinery.
- Mirelle: records, false seals, route contradictions.
- Corren: grapple, restrain, drag civilians, secure prisoners.
- Rook: morale, child/witness grounding, absurd table-memory beat.
- Ves: scouting, sting-disabler, threshold reaction.

## South Lantern Contact Options

Use one if pacing needs a visible villain:

1. Bell contact: South Lantern speaks through the relay bell.
2. Mask contact: the command mask repeats or resists a phrase.
3. Cane sign: black cane mark or unlit lantern pin appears before she does.
4. Parley: she offers records or prisoners in exchange for Pella, evidence, or withdrawal.
5. Abandonment: she cuts off one of her own agents because frightened people are easier to move than failed people.

South Lantern should recognize:

- Brannok makes civilians brave.
- Selene understands thresholds and choices.
- Pella matters, but not as an object.

## What To Avoid

- Do not turn Bellweather into only a document dungeon.
- Do not make the south bell an instant unsignaled gotcha.
- Do not force Pella into danger.
- Do not have companions solve the main problem while Brannok/Selene watch.
- Do not let paperwork replace people in danger.
- Do not reveal DM secrets through NPCs who would not know them.
