# DM Secrets

Future plans are DM guidance, not current player-facing truth. Do not reveal directly. Do not force outcomes. Do not let NPCs know future-plan information unless they have an in-world reason.

## Confirmed Direction

The Last Lantern is a character-driven road-rescue fantasy.

Core thesis:

Powerful people use roads and records to decide who gets abandoned. The Last Lantern becomes the company that refuses to leave people behind.

The villains should use understandable methods: route closure, false ward-iron, custody orders, witness seizure, compromised guards, closed relay houses, and official-looking pressure.

## Villain Plans

Orvaine Thresh and the acceptable-loss network want to keep old routing systems from becoming public proof. They may:

- collapse routes to force “necessary” sacrifices;
- seize witnesses;
- destroy ledgers, route marks, custody chains, or protected testimony;
- use shelters, children, prisoners, road-workers, and refugees as leverage;
- frame atrocity as logistics;
- use paper when knives fail.

## South Lantern Plans

South Lantern should become visible soon. She is the field face of the first arc.

She may:

- speak through a relay bell;
- appear under parley;
- abandon one of her own field teams;
- threaten witness custody through official channels;
- recognize Brannok as dangerous because he makes civilians brave;
- recognize Selene as dangerous because she understands thresholds and consequence.

## Secrets Not Yet Earned

- South Lantern's real name, if any is discoverable.
- Whether S.L./L.S. is a title, initials, station mark, or deliberate misdirection.
- The full shape of O. Thresh's authority.
- Which Greybridge people beyond Dain Vorther are compromised.
- What Old Bellweather House still holds.
- What Selene's patron wants from threshold crossings.

## Conditions For Reveal

Reveal hidden information through:

- rescued witnesses;
- captured agents;
- physical route devices;
- public consequence scenes;
- hard choices under time pressure;
- evidence that points to people and danger, not abstract puzzle law.

## Things Not To Rush

- Brannok and Selene trusting each other.
- Daughters and Brannok's allies becoming a living cast.
- Thresh appearing in full.
- Any romantic, emotional, or grief closure.
- Selene fully understanding her patron.
- Corren becoming too competent too quickly.

## Bellweather House Requirements

Bellweather House must contain rescuable victims, not only documents.

Recommended objective:

Rescue trapped people, recover South Lantern's field ledger, and prevent the relay bell from sending a false route order before South Lantern burns the evidence.

Include:

- burned wing from old structural fire
- sealed Road Works archive room
- ward relay chamber
- bell tower or signal chamber
- smuggler route / old service tunnel
- false safety markers
- unlit lantern symbol
- tactical fight near bell/relay
- hazard where breaking the wrong thing destroys evidence or harms captives
- character-specific spotlight opportunities

## Threads To Plant Subtly

- The network has studied Brannok's protective priorities.
- South Lantern knows Brannok makes civilians brave.
- South Lantern knows Selene understands doors/thresholds.
- Refugee children and ordinary workers may carry the most important testimony.
- The safest-looking route may be the one designed to fail.
