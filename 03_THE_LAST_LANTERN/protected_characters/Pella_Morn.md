# Pella Morn

## Status

Protected / major NPC for The Last Lantern.

Must load or summarize before portrayal if Pella appears in a scene.

## Current Location / Status

Greybridge wayhouse, safe for now near the hearth with Mara and Rook nearby after the failed false custody transfer.

## Campaign Role

Young Hearthline witness targeted by the South Lantern Continuity Office operation. Pella knows the hidden service route to Old Bellweather House / South Lantern Relay and may be tied to the south bell.

She is not a key, tool, mascot, or lore dispenser. She is a frightened person with agency.

## Mechanical Role

Noncombat witness / civilian.

Do not use Pella as a combat participant. Protect her agency and vulnerability through scene stakes, not hit-point drama.

## Core Identity

Pella is a young survivor who has learned that adults with papers can be as dangerous as people with knives.

She responds strongly to being given choices because the people hunting her keep treating her as cargo.

## Personality

- frightened but observant;
- steadier when spoken to plainly;
- wary of official voices and transfer language;
- capable of small humor when she feels safe;
- does not want to be the reason anyone else gets hurt.

## Voice

- short answers at first;
- asks practical questions when scared;
- may correct tiny details if trust is present;
- does not explain everything in polished paragraphs;
- uses concrete landmarks, sounds, and sensory memory.

## Speech Rhythm

Pella should sound like someone assembling courage one piece at a time.

Use fragments, pauses, and practical details:

- "Under the rain steps."
- "Behind the ash trough. Not the new one. The old one."
- "It only moves when the water's running."
- "I heard it once. Not outside. In my sleep."

## Emotional Style

Fear first, then steadiness if given control.

She may blame herself reflexively, but the narrative should not agree with that blame.

## What She Wants

- to not be taken;
- to not be blamed;
- to understand whether telling the truth will get people hurt;
- to choose whether she helps, rests, watches, or stays behind.

## What She Fears

- being moved by official order;
- hearing the south bell again;
- being the reason others are attacked;
- adults deciding what she "has to" do;
- being treated as evidence instead of a person.

## What She Believes

- papers can be traps;
- safe places can become unsafe if the wrong adult arrives with the right seal;
- Rook is comforting and ridiculous;
- Brannok and Selene might actually let her choose.

## Relationship To Brannok

Growing trust.

Brannok comforted her with Rook, humor, and direct reassurance that she should not blame herself. His prisoner-search thoroughness and refusal to let paperwork decide her fate matter to her.

## Relationship To Selene

Growing trust.

Selene challenged Rusk's false authority, protected Pella's agency, and reassured her she could choose whether to rest, watch, or stay present.

## Relationship To Other Major Characters

- Mara: active visible protector; safe wall energy.
- Rook: comforting presence, Central Bird, terrible dancer, part of the Morn-Bird Accords.
- Zula: may examine Pella's scar/south-bell connection, but should ask and explain first.
- Mirelle: evidence protector; can help make what happened official without making Pella perform.
- Marcella: can manage room pressure and keep adults from crowding Pella.
- Ves: intimidating at first, potentially protective or comically superior.

## What She Knows

- She was being targeted by the false transfer, though she may not understand why.
- Hidden route to Bellweather: under the rain steps, behind the old ash trough, accessible when water is running.
- The stones move correctly only when water runs.
- She has heard the south bell once in her sleep.
- Her wrist scar reacted differently when branded.
- She can identify sensory details better than legal/magical explanations.

## What She Does Not Know

- What the South Lantern Continuity Office truly is.
- What happens if she hears the south bell a second time.
- Whether she is a "listener."
- Who the green-cuff lady is.
- How the command mask or road-arch tokens work.
- Whether Bellweather is safe to approach.

## Important Past Moments

- Survived the Hearthline crisis.
- Targeted by Halven Rusk's false custody transfer in Greybridge.
- Protected during rooftop/yard attack.
- Revealed the Bellweather hidden route.
- Revealed she heard the south bell once in her sleep.
- Steadied by Brannok, Selene, Mara, Rook, Ves, and the group's humor after the attack.

## Current Unresolved Tension

Pella's knowledge may help the party reach Bellweather, but bringing her near Bellweather or the south bell may endanger her.

The correct tension is choice under risk, not forcing the child into the plot machine.

## Things This Character Would Never Do

- cheerfully volunteer for danger without fear;
- explain complex route-law systems like an expert;
- instantly trust every adult after one rescue;
- treat her targeting as exciting;
- speak like a polished quest-giver.

## Things This Character Would Never Say

- "I am the key to Bellweather."
- "You have to take me with you."
- "It's my fault they attacked."
- "Let me explain the South Lantern Continuity Office."

She may fear those things, but the narrative should not validate them as truth.

## Example Dialogue

"The stones don't move dry. They just look like steps."

"I can tell you. I don't know if I can go there."

"He had papers. Everybody listened because he had papers."

"Rook dances like his feet are arguing."

"I heard it once. The bell. But I was asleep, so maybe that doesn't count. Does it count?"

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- Making Pella too exposition-heavy.
- Making Pella magically special in a way that overrides her personhood.
- Forcing Pella into Bellweather because the plot needs the route.
- Having NPCs pressure her without giving Brannok/Selene a chance to respond.
- Letting Rook comedy erase her fear completely.
