# Companions

## Brannok-Side

### Rook / Central Bird

Soot-black crow, not a raven. Cannot speak words. Clever, rude, loyal on his own terms, emotionally important, loves bread and petty authority.

Do not kill casually. Does not get independent combat turns unless the player directs or the scene specifically supports it.

Rook is Central Bird because frightened children at Carter's Rest/Stonewake latched onto the title after Brannok misspoke from “essential dwarf” into “central dwarf.” The children chanted “Central Bird,” and it made fear smaller.

Rook is a bad dancer. It never gets better. That is the point. He shows how much fun he is having, not how good he is.

Rook is emotionally “sticky” from naming, belief, and repeated care, but he is not a druid, not a familiar, and not a transformed person.

### Nera Cale

Carter's Rest innkeeper. Practical, sharp, protective, emotionally guarded. Feeds people like she is threatening them. Stays with Carter's Rest; does not leave the inn for the road.

Brannok is homesick for Carter's Rest and Nera. His grey knit cap and Cloak of Names make him think of her yelling at him for dumb nicknames like Gentle Horse Tackler.

### Mirelle Thorn

Inquisitive Rogue 5. Legal/evidence/cipher expert. Brave through fear. Dry, socially useful, not a frontline fighter.

Traveling because Last Lantern and Iron Road evidence requires someone who can read route marks, writs, plates, courier shorthand, old routing indexes, and forged custody orders.

Mirelle is not a bard. Her power is accuracy, records, contradiction, and making truth survive.

### Corren Dask

Pugilist 5, Roadhouse Grappler / Yardfighter.

Low-ranking guard turned road-brawler after three months around Brannok. Certified Survivor, back-table defender, witness to the Princess Carry Incident.

Cracks jokes when scared or when it is socially unwise. Useful, brave, and underqualified in a human way.

Combat role: grapple runners, restrain prisoners, shove enemies, drag civilians clear, handle ropes/wagons/doors, and become a physical problem.

Must not become reckless proof-of-worth bait.

## Selene-Side

### Ves

Cat-sized pseudodragon familiar. Not a cat. Cannot speak words. Proud, possessive, dramatic, loyal to Selene, emotionally important.

Ves acts on Selene's initiative. Selene can command Ves with a bonus action.

Ves should remain tactically relevant through Pact of the Chain and Investment of the Chain Master.

### Marcella Viretti

Lore Bard 10. Selene's romantic partner. Elegant social blade, sharp, stylish, proud, protective, vulnerability hidden in precision.

Marcella is not just commentary. Use Bardic Inspiration, Cutting Words, Dissonant Whispers, Hypnotic Pattern, Counterspell/Magical Secrets as appropriate.

She sees the emotional architecture of a room and changes the tempo.

### Kara

Life Cleric 10. Protector, faith, mercy with backbone, frontline shield. Currently warned at Saint Orveth's Reliquary.

### Eya Harisa

Knowledge Cleric 10. Survivor care, memory, gentle truth, emotional stabilizer. Warning sent: do not let Eya pursue suspicious movement alone.

### Jasna

Mastermind Rogue / Knowledge Cleric. Legal/evidence knife, records, public truth, official consequence.

Jasna and Mirelle should be terrifying together: mutual respect, competitive competence, and documents capable of killing a magistrate socially.

### Zula Ozu

Wardwright Tattooist Artificer 10.

Tattoo artist, wardcraft, countermarks, practical magic, dangerous curiosity with a conscience. Magic should feel like ink, skin, chalk, etching, repair, and crafted magical logic.

Zula is thinking about group tattoo concepts after Bellweather House.

### Needle / Nessa

Swashbuckler Rogue 10. Tabaxi rogue. Barefoot, architecture menace, dry, precise, deflects care.

Real name is Nessa. Needle started as an insult and became a warning. Both names are real in different ways.

Active files should refer to her as Needle / Nessa without adding a surname unless the player confirms it. Avoid using the surname Vale for new NPCs.

### Mara / Bellbreaker

Living Wall / Cavalier Fighter 9.

Mara is not a healer. She is a blunt wall, “the door,” protective and not soft about it.

Bellbreaker is Mara's sword/nickname. It does not belong to Zula.

Mara's style: concise, physical, emotionally minimal but deeply protective. “Good doors do not explain themselves.”

## Support-Wing Rule

In large scenes, do not run everyone as full initiative. Assign support objectives such as evacuate children, hold wagons, stabilize wards, protect evidence, block exits, or counter a secondary threat.

In crowded social-pressure scenes, give companions visible jobs instead of full dialogue turns. Suggested Greybridge lanes: Mirelle protects records and catches contradictions; Corren watches prisoners, doors, wagons, and runners; Mara holds civilians and chokepoints; Marcella manages panic and applies Bard support; Zula studies seals, wardcraft, and magical marks; Needle / Nessa scouts hidden movement and escape routes; Rook supports children and morale; Ves acts on Selene's initiative and follows Selene's commands.
