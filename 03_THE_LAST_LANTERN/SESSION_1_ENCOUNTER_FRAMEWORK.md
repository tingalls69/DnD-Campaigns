# Session 1 Encounter Framework

Use this to run the South Lantern Waystation opening without handwaving the mechanics.

## Core Rule

All three threats matter. The player chooses priorities. Success does not require solving everything perfectly; it requires choosing and living with consequences.

Ask what Brannok and Selene do separately if needed. Do not assume they agree.

## Initiative Handling

Option A: If the player moves immediately and no one attacks a PC yet, run the first exchange as simultaneous crisis actions, then roll initiative when violence becomes direct.

Option B: If the false relief worker attacks, roll initiative immediately. Include only active participants near the records wagon plus any PC/ally who moves there.

Option C: For a large split scene, use group initiative by zone:

- Ward-post zone.
- Wagon-line zone.
- Records-wagon zone.
- Civilian support zone.

## Battlefield Zones

### North Edge - Cracked Ward-Post

Broken ward-stakes, pale fire, unstable ground, panicking guards, pressure from outside the defensive line.

### East Wagon Line - Mud/Sinkhole

Witness/children wagon, screaming horses, wheel dragged down, civilians trapped or slipping, sabotaged roadwork under mud.

### Records Wagon

Sealed testimony chest, crates, false relief worker, nearby witnesses, Mirelle's papers, potential escape route through canvas kitchens.

### Camp Center

Cookfires, children, Rook/Ves chaos, Commander Rhea Varric if needed, overwhelmed local healer.

## Clocks

| Threat | Clock | Advance When | Failure Consequence |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Ward-post failure | 0/4 | ignored, failed ward checks, heavy impact near post | breach opens; road-hungry creatures or monsters can enter |
| Wagon sinkhole | 0/3 | ignored, failed rescue checks, horses panic | wagon tips; civilians injured/trapped; Hearthline mobility damaged |
| Records attack | 0/2 | ignored, attacker wins opposed action, no one guards chest | testimony chest damaged/stolen; witness or evidence targeted |
| Civilian panic | 0/5 | visible injury, breach, fire, ignored children/witnesses | crowd surges; movement becomes difficult; additional saves/checks needed |

Reduce or pause clocks when the protagonists assign competent allies, spend resources, or solve the immediate cause.

## Suggested DCs

- DC 12: calm a small group, spot obvious danger, help a stuck civilian.
- DC 14: identify sabotage marks, stabilize a horse team, protect evidence under pressure.
- DC 15: brace the wagon, read the ward-post failure, keep a crowd from surging.
- DC 16: interrupt the false relief worker cleanly, contain the ward flare, locate the sinkhole's sabotage point.
- DC 18: solve a threat quickly enough to prevent clock advance this round.

Use advantage when a character's established identity strongly fits the action: Brannok physically protecting a wagon, Selene forcing consequence/witness magic, Zula reading wardcraft, Needle spotting sabotage, Mirelle reading route documents, Mara/Corren holding a line, Marcella controlling a social panic.

## Threat A - Ward-Post Failure

Best suited to Zula, Selene, Mirelle assisting with ward notes, or Brannok if the solution becomes physical.

Useful actions:

- Arcana DC 15 to understand the failure.
- Investigation DC 15 to spot deliberate tampering.
- Strength/Athletics DC 16 to brace or move a physical support under pressure.
- Dispel Magic or similar resource can stop one clock advance or force a contested check against the sabotage.
- Creative Selene magic may expose a hidden name/seal/authorization tied to the tampering.

## Threat B - Wagon Line Collapse

Best suited to Brannok, Mara, Corren, Selene, or Needle.

Useful actions:

- Athletics DC 15 to brace wheel/axle or haul civilians clear.
- Animal Handling DC 14 to keep horses from worsening the collapse.
- Investigation or Perception DC 16 to find the sabotage point.
- Rope, crowbar, hammer, telekinesis, or battlefield-control magic can reduce the clock.

## Threat C - Records Wagon Attack

Best suited to Needle, Marcella, Mirelle, Selene, or Corren.

False relief worker baseline:

- Use Spy or Bandit Captain as a quick base depending desired danger.
- Recommended opening version: Spy-like infiltrator, AC 12-14, HP 27-45, dagger/shortsword, Deception +5 or better, Sleight of Hand +5 or better.
- Do not make them a robed cultist. They should look like the help.

Useful actions:

- Perception/Insight DC 15 to catch the attack before the first strike.
- Grapple or shove to stop the attacker from reaching the chest.
- Sleight of Hand/Thieves' Tools DC 15 to secure the testimony chest under pressure.
- Persuasion/Intimidation/Deception DC 16 to force hesitation or expose the disguise publicly.

## Consequences By Round If Ignored

### After 1 Round

- Ward-post: pale fire spreads to nearby stakes.
- Wagon: one wheel sinks halfway; children/witnesses scream; horses panic.
- Records: attacker reaches chest or wounds a guard/witness.

### After 2 Rounds

- Ward-post: defensive line flickers; something outside tests the gap.
- Wagon: wagon tilts; first civilian injury or trapped passenger.
- Records: chest lock damaged, seal broken, or attacker starts escape with packet.

### After 3 Rounds

- Ward-post: breach opens unless contained.
- Wagon: wagon tips or axle breaks.
- Records: testimony is stolen/damaged unless stopped.

## Ending Targets

End Session 1 after one:

1. The Hearthline survives the first attack and must choose a route.
2. A captured infiltrator reveals the caravan was deliberately routed into danger.
3. Brannok and Selene have their first real conversation after seeing each other act.
4. The road ahead lights with failing ward-fire, making clear the caravan cannot go back.
