# Story Bible

## Premise

The Hearthline refugee crossing is moving civilians, witnesses, children, injured workers, rescued prisoners, and fragile evidence away from failing roads and corrupt systems. Brannok and Selene arrive from different finished victories to protect the same living crisis.

## Core Conflict

Orvaine Thresh and the acceptable-loss network believe civilization requires choosing who can be sacrificed, routed, erased, renamed, or left behind. Brannok and Selene are proof that ordinary people can refuse that logic.

## Themes

- No one gets left behind unless we choose to leave them.
- Names and roads both decide who is allowed to exist in public.
- False safety kills.
- Evidence matters because people matter.
- Protection is not control.
- Victory should create responsibility, not erase consequence.

## Tone

Grounded fantasy road crisis with tactical danger, emotional stakes, recurring humor, chosen family, institutional cruelty, and table-memory callbacks.

## Expected Campaign Shape

### Act I - The Hearthline Crossing

The caravan must cross dangerous roads while factions try to seize people, destroy records, or collapse the route.

### Act II - The Town That Was Chosen To Fail

A settlement is being quietly sacrificed to preserve more profitable routes. The heroes must decide whether to expose, evacuate, fight, split forces, or all of those at once.

### Act III - The Acceptable Loss Engine

The party confronts the living system behind Thresh's choices: officials, old road hardware, compliant nobles, route agents, and terrified workers.

## Guardrails

- Keep the story human before it is conspiratorial.
- Use lore as leverage for choices, not as a lecture.
- Do not make Thresh metaphysical. He is a person and a system-builder.
- The crossover should honor both completed solo finales.
