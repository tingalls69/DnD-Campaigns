# Story Bible

## Premise

The Hearthline refugee crossing is moving civilians, witnesses, children, injured workers, rescued prisoners, and fragile evidence away from failing roads and corrupt systems. Brannok and Selene arrived from different finished victories to protect the same living crisis.

The campaign is now in the Greybridge-to-Bellweather phase: the first direct custody-theft attempt has failed, Pella Morn has been identified as a targeted witness, and Old Bellweather House / South Lantern Relay is the next major site.

## Core Conflict

Orvaine Thresh and the acceptable-loss network believe civilization requires choosing who can be sacrificed, routed, erased, renamed, or left behind. Brannok and Selene are proof that ordinary people can refuse that logic.

## Themes

- No one gets left behind unless we choose to leave them.
- Names and roads both decide who is allowed to exist in public.
- False safety kills.
- Evidence matters because people matter.
- Protection is not control.
- Victory should create responsibility, not erase consequence.
- Official paperwork can be a weapon, but the story must stay about people.

## Tone

Grounded fantasy road crisis with tactical danger, emotional stakes, recurring humor, chosen family, institutional cruelty, and table-memory callbacks.

## Current Campaign Shape

### Act I - Greybridge: Paper First, Knife Second

Status: mostly completed.

The Hearthline survived physical attack and custody theft. The party captured Halven Rusk and the masked coordinator alive, secured evidence, protected Pella Morn, and learned the Bellweather hidden route.

Remaining Act I play is fallout: interrogation, evidence review, Pella's choices, Greybridge security, and deciding how to approach Bellweather.

### Act II - Bellweather Approach

The party chooses how to reach Old Bellweather House / South Lantern Relay.

The strongest current route is Pella's hidden servant-road entrance beneath the rain steps and behind the old ash trough. It works when water runs and the stones move correctly.

This act should include practical danger, not only planning: route hazards, watchers, false ward markers, a guide problem, or a cleanup team trying to erase the failed Greybridge trail.

### Act III - Bellweather House: The Relay That Still Rings

Bellweather is the first major adventure site. It must contain rescuable people, not only documents.

The goals are to rescue trapped people, recover South Lantern's field ledger or relay evidence, and prevent the south bell / relay from sending or completing another false route order.

## Guardrails

- Keep the story human before it is conspiratorial.
- Use lore as leverage for choices, not as a lecture.
- Do not make Thresh metaphysical. He is a person and a system-builder.
- Do not treat Pella as a key, tool, or lore dispenser.
- Do not force Brannok and Selene into agreement.
- The crossover should honor both completed solo finales.
