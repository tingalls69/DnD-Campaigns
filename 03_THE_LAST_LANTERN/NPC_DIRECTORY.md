# NPC Directory

## Magistrate Elowen

Greybridge magistrate. Competent, cautious, and practical. She is not automatically trusted, but she has acted responsibly so far.

She understands archive fire rules, emergency courier authority, and the danger of official-looking false transfer orders.

## Sergeant Brindle Korran

Greybridge night-watch sergeant. Practical, broad-shouldered, tired-eyed, and very unhappy to discover possible rot inside his own gate detail.

Use this name for the Greybridge sergeant. Do not use Rusk.

## Taman

Greybridge clerk assisting Magistrate Elowen. Nervous but useful. Helped retrieve sealed Road Works indexes with Corren watching.

## Pell

Cooperating witness. Former message carrier/low-level participant who ran because they were scared. Should not be treated as fully innocent or as hardened villain.

Pell knows pieces of the South Lantern chain and carried a message to Old Bellweather House.

## Dain Vorther

Compromised green-cuff auxiliary in Greybridge. Fled toward south stable / old ice-cellar escape route and was caught by Needle and Ves.

## Commander Rhea Varric

Leader of the Hearthline refugee crossing. Ex-soldier turned evacuation commander. Tired, funny when cornered, brutally practical, no patience for heroes making speeches while wagons are stuck.

## Orvaine Thresh / O. Thresh

Institutional villain. Not a god and not a concept. A person who believes civilization requires choosing acceptable losses.

Thresh uses old Road Works authority, route closures, custody transfers, sealed indexes, false safety designations, and official paperwork to make vulnerable people disappear while everything looks legal.

His horror is that he can close a road on paper and make people vanish in the real world.

## South Lantern

Immediate visible villain and field commander/title/identity tied to the unlit lantern/crossed-flame symbol.

Known description:

- Older woman, perhaps late forties or older.
- Tall, straight-backed.
- Black and silver hair braided close to the scalp.
- Dark road coat, plain but well-made.
- Gloves.
- Gray iron lantern pin at the collar, depicting an empty/unlit lantern frame.
- Thin white scar under jaw/side of throat.
- Black wood cane with silver cap.
- Silver cane cap engraved with an unlit lantern/crossed-out flame.
- Tiny letters under the cane mark: S.L. or L.S.
- Speaks like someone trained in old road-office authority.
- Does not shout; people believe her because she sounds inevitable.

Known phrases:

- “Lanterns are for those who fear the dark.”
- “Roads obey the hand that closes them.”

South Lantern believes roads do not need light; they need obedience. She believes frightened people are easier to move. She recognizes that Brannok makes civilians brave and that Selene understands thresholds.

She should appear or speak soon. Do not keep her only as a rumor for too long.

## Rell Vant

Captured Stonewake route auditor / logistics officer. Local expression of acceptable-loss ideology. Alive, gagged/chained/guarded. Not the final villain.

## Cressa Durn / Cressida Rosevein

Defeated Vellford villain, in custody. Survivor of erasure and perpetrator of erasure. Do not reopen the Vellford finale as unresolved.

## Tessa Rill

Rescued witness/survivor from Vellford. Red-haired laundry worker, erased as wage-book fraud, alive.

## Annet Crowle

Rescued Ashcombe House housekeeper/key-holder/witness.

## Merrit Vane

Rescued teenage witness, reunited with mother Lysa.

## Lysa Vane

Merrit's mother, survivor, reunited with Merrit.

## Hearthline Children

Not plot devices. A recurring chorus of fear, jokes, bad questions, Rook fandom, Ves worship, and reminders that the road matters because people live on it.

## Naming Rule

Avoid common/colliding surnames like Vale and Rusk for new NPCs. Prefer distinctive names that are easy to track and not similar to main characters or major NPCs.
