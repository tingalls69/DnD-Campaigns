# Protagonists

## Brannok Ashhelm

Mountain dwarf Champion Fighter 8. Hammer-and-shield protector. Practical, blunt, perceptive, morally serious, and not stupid.

### Current Build

- Class: Fighter 8
- Subclass: Champion
- XP: 46,900 / 48,000
- HP: 84/84
- AC: 18
- STR 20, DEX 14, CON 18, INT 12, WIS 15, CHA 8
- Fighting Style: Dueling
- Feats: Crusher, Sentinel, Skill Expert: Athletics
- HP method: average HP going forward

### Voice

- Plain speech.
- Work, food, roads, hammers, nails, rats, weasels, carts, and bad examples.
- Jokes when uncomfortable.
- Serious words should be simple and heavy.
- Homesickness often emerges as jokes, food references, or references to Carter's Rest/Nera rather than direct confession.

### Core Wound

Brannok saw signs in the mines, waited too long, and people died. He will not wait again.

### Current Truth

Brannok defended Carter's Rest, saved children/witnesses/ledger, captured Rell Vant alive, exposed Iron Road / Last Lantern handoff threads, helped save the Hearthline, and arrived in Greybridge with the witnesses alive.

He is wearing the grey knit cap and Cloak of Names. He is homesick for Carter's Rest and Nera.

### Growth Arc

Brannok's growth is not learning to care. He already cares deeply.

His arc is learning that helping people does not require disappearing into the work, and that others can carry part of the burden without the light going out.

Bellweather House should test him with more than one urgent thing to protect at once, forcing a choice about what he personally holds and what he trusts others to handle.

### Symbols

- Grey Knit Cap of the Rest
- Cloak of Names, nonmagical but emotionally central
- Road-marker tattoo concept: weathered post, carved survivor initials, hammer and shield at base, crow above, small lantern mark worked into the post

### Never Portray Him As

- A dumb brute.
- A random violence machine.
- Comic relief first.
- Someone whose hammer chooses for him.
- Someone who does not understand emotional stakes.

## Selene Vaer

Archfey Warlock 10, Pact of the Chain. Dramatic, wounded, sharp, funny, dangerous, vulnerable, and devoted to chosen family.

### Current Build

- Class: Warlock 10
- Subclass: Archfey
- Pact: Pact of the Chain
- XP: 77,750 / 85,000
- HP: 75/75
- Pact slots: 2/2 after long rest
- Pact slots are 5th-level
- Spell save DC: 17
- Spell attack: +9
- Familiar: Ves, pseudodragon

### Patron Revision

Selene's old name-magic / true-name / anti-erasure homebrew patron mechanics are deprecated for the active campaign.

She is now an Archfey Warlock flavored around thresholds, masks, bargains, fear, charm, crossings, consequence, hidden rooms, and roads that lead where they should not.

Working patron names:

- The Lady Beyond the Threshold
- The Court Behind the Closed Eye
- The Court Beyond the Closed Door

The patron should not feel like a standard fairy queen. It should feel like an old power that lives behind doors people cannot quite remember opening.

### Closed-Eye Charm

Selene's closed-eye charm remains exactly what it was: a handmade silver closed-eye necklace from her mother, no gems or ornament.

It is not from the patron. It is not patron branding. It is family, identity, memory, and emotional protection.

The patron noticed the symbol; the patron did not create it.

### Voice

- Stylish, direct when needed, theatrical under pressure.
- Humor and elegance can become weapons.
- Emotion may arrive as deflection, precision, or sudden honesty.
- She can be terrifying without being cruel.

### Core Wound

People tried to erase, own, rename, and use Selene. She refuses to be unmade and fights for witnesses to matter.

### Current Truth

Selene's Vellford finale is complete. She destroyed the Old Rose Conservatory apparatus, saved living anchors, exposed Cressa Durn, and chose consequence/prevention over revenge.

### Growth Arc

Selene's old arc was: You cannot erase me.

Her Last Lantern arc is: I decide what happens after you try.

Her question is: Can Selene become consequence without becoming control?

She should face moments where fear/charm would be easy, but offering real choice is better.

### Patron Scene Guidance

At Bellweather House, give Selene a threshold scene. She finds a door where no door should be. Ves refuses to enter first. The closed-eye charm goes cold, then warm. Behind the door is a room that feels part parlor, part courtroom, part backstage. Empty chairs face Selene.

Suggested patron line:

> No. You brought the door, Selene Vaer. I only taught it to open.

The patron may tempt, reveal, or offer, but must not command Selene or define her feelings.

### Never Portray Her As

- Generic seductive warlock.
- Emotionally fluent in every moment.
- Someone who abandons the Daughters for plot convenience.
- Someone who needs Brannok to validate her victory.

## Crossover Dynamic

Brannok and Selene should challenge each other without either being diminished. They may misunderstand each other at first, but both should quickly recognize that the other protects people when it costs something.

Brannok protects by moving first. Selene protects by making consequences unavoidable.
