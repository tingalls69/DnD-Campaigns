# 10 Combat NPC Sheets and Companion Plan

Purpose: recurring Crowstep/Stonewake NPCs who may appear in field operations, escorts, or tactical scenes. These are support sheets, not replacements for Brannok as protagonist.

## Use Rules
- Brannok/player rolls all Brannok dice personally.
- Use visible Python for all NPC/enemy/ally/environment rolls.
- NPCs should only use abilities that fit their knowledge, motives, and position.
- Do not let NPCs solve Brannok's core objective while he watches.
- If an NPC participates in combat, show AC, HP, weapons, and role before the scene gets tactical.
- Keep NPC knowledge boundaries strict.

---

## Sergeant Elian Voss
- Martial commander / field arrest lead / investigator.
- Role: tactical pressure and lawful authority.
- Use him when arrest, escorts, and public authority matter.
- Strong on battlefield control and finishing wounded enemies.
- He trusts Brannok as a rough public menace who gets results.

## Kaelis
- Bow-focused field ally and witness-protector.
- Role: overwatch, precision shots, civilian protection.
- She should choose field involvement naturally, not be forced into a long-term role.
- If she is in the fight, she works best by eliminating ranged threats, covering civilians, and holding flanks.

## Mirelle Thorn
- Legal / ledger / evidence ally.
- Role: paper, custody, seal, and turning clues into one actionable connection.
- Best used off-screen for the boring evidence work, then brought in with the useful result.
- She is not a frontliner by default.

## Nera
- Carter's Rest innkeeper.
- Role: home base, moral pressure, food, and terrifying common sense.
- Not a combat party member, but she is the center of the safe-home anchor.
- If the story requires Nera in a fight, it should be rare and special.

## Tovin Brack
- Half-dwarf / dwarven-blood wardwright.
- Role: ward lantern, chalk lines, road-ward logic, repair specialist.
- AC / HP should be assigned if a tactical scene needs them. Default him as support first.
- He is not a frontliner, but he is crucial when the road is lying.

## Pellan Orsik
- Dwarf smith and honest iron worker.
- Role: clamp, hammer, brute repair, old road metal knowledge.
- If combat happens, he can hold a line and smash things, but his real value is in repair and terrain interaction.
- Good default battlefield role: close support, object-breaking, clamp-setting.

## Garrow
- Old road-binder / road-closure worker.
- Role: dangerous leveraged helper / prisoner / eventual witness.
- He knows bell-locks, old road routes, and how the sabotage worked.
- If he is used in a tactical scene, he should be treated as an unstable ally under guard.

## Generic Ward Guards / Road Guards
- Use them as support bodies: hold civilians, control horses, block exits, or keep a line.
- They should not steal Brannok's spotlight.
- They exist to make the road and Ward Office feel like an organization, not a solo act.

## Suggested future ally candidates
If the story needs a more clearly classed long-term companion later, introduce one through action and emotional stakes rather than exposition. The companion should be combat-capable and able to travel with Brannok for moral reasons, not just paperwork.
