# 05 NPCs, Factions, and Relationships

## Core Allies

### Rook
Soot-black crow. Clever, rude, loyal on his own terms. Cannot speak words. Scouts, croaks, warns, steals crumbs, and reacts to road-ward magic like he understands more than he admits. Running joke: secret shapeshifter druid, but not canon unless the story truly proves otherwise.

### Nera
Carter's Rest innkeeper. Gruff, sharp, practical, and more dangerous than most people in the room realize. She feeds Brannok, scolds him, and keeps his room and chest safe. She wants the inn intact and hates nonsense, especially from birds and furniture-destroying dwarves.

### Voss
Sergeant Elian Voss. Ward sergeant, practical investigator, lawful pressure valve. He trusts Brannok as a rough public-menace ally because Brannok keeps saving lives and evidence. He is the main Ward authority on the ground.

### Mirelle
Legal/ledger ally. She understands the paper side of corruption and is best used to turn one recognized name, mark, or contradiction into an actionable conclusion. She is strongest when she brings back one useful connection instead of a pile of unrelated clues.

### Kaelis
Bow ally and practical field witness. She has become an important combat partner and emotional witness-protector. She is not forced into party membership, but when she is in the field she works cleanly and competently.

### Tovin Brack
Half-dwarf / dwarven-blood wardwright. Practical old-road expert. Uses blue ward lanterns, chalk, and tools. Not a frontliner by default, but extremely valuable when road-ward magic is involved. Currently central to the final local road-ward repairs.

### Pellan Orsik
Dwarf smith. Blunt, reliable, and competent in both a fight and in old road-gate metalwork. Handles clamps, hammer-set repairs, and ugly but honest fixes. He and Brannok have a growing working relationship.

### Garrow
Old road-binder / emergency road-closure worker. Dangerous, guilty, useful, and under tethered supervision. He knows bell-locks, old road work, and how the sabotage system was put together. He is not trusted, but he is not ignored.

### Nera / Pellan / Tovin / Voss / Kaelis status
They are the current practical field team. Their jobs:
- Voss: lawful pressure and arrests
- Mirelle: evidence / legal connections
- Tovin: wardcraft / lantern / chalk / repair logic
- Pellan: clamps / hammer / physical repair
- Kaelis: overwatch / protection / precise shots
- Nera: home base / food / sanity / intimidation

## Key Antagonists and Local Threats

### Halven
Deputy assessor. Delayed and distorted claims, including Brannok's. His office connected to the red ledger and route delays.

### Marra Valech
Certified route/audit figure. Linked to false safety, route certification, and the road paperwork that made bad repairs look official.

### Road-mage Binder
A field road-writer / road-controller who worked the bell-lock and false road-opening mechanics. Currently the local field threat at the old road system.

### Gray-coated route woman
Survey-store route/cache handler. Guarded ward-iron wedges and route material at the old survey store.

### Tarp-covered stitched shape
An unnatural, stitched, ward-adjacent thing hidden in the survey store. It is one of the higher-fantasy signals that the world is changing.

### Rell Garrow
Older road-binder whose name matters because he knows old road-warden work and the broader network. He is a thread that points outward from Stonewake.

### Carrow
Not yet fully resolved in the play, but his ledger and routing marks are important. This is the kind of name that matters because it connects old road-warden accounting to the local rot.

## Relationship Notes
- Brannok and Nera are home-base / protective authority.
- Brannok and Voss are trusted field allies.
- Brannok and Mirelle are the paper-and-hammer duo.
- Brannok and Kaelis have become a strong practical combat pair.
- Brannok and Pellan share blunt dwarf-to-dwarf road-and-metal understanding.
- Brannok and Tovin share the old road-ward logic of “if the brace goes cold, something is wrong.”
- Garrow is useful but dangerous; keep him under guard and never let him forget that he is not trusted.
