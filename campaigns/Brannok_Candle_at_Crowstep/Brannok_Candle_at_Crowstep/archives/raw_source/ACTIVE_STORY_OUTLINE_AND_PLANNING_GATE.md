# Active Story Outline and Planning Gate - Brannok / Candle at Crowstep

DM-FACING. Use this document to keep future play planned, simple, and consistent. Do not reveal hidden future beats unless discovered in play.

## Current Campaign Assessment
Brannok's choices have shifted the campaign away from dense occult paperwork and toward a practical road-protection adventure. This is the correct active direction.

The local Crowstep Crest operation has been smashed open. Brannok has now left Crowstep and is on the first night of travel north toward Stonewake Market.

## Current Save Point
Brannok is at the North Road wayhouse, sleeping in Dessa Marn's wagon after recovering a stolen wagon/cargo from the old tollhouse. The wayhouse is guarded. The green-lantern buyer fled north/side-track and remains the active Stonewake-facing lead.

If the rest is uninterrupted, next scene should be morning at the wayhouse, resources restored, with the road to Stonewake ahead.

## What Brannok Has Changed
- Broke the immediate Marrow/Third Bell control-room threat and stabilized the road wards long enough for honest repairs.
- Exposed Crest Ledger's local profiteering scheme faster and more aggressively than the original outline expected.
- Captured/delivered multiple Crest-linked criminals alive, producing strong legal evidence.
- Negotiated with Skrit's goblins, creating a possible labor/road-scout contact.
- Rescued Tovan Reed and demonstrated public protection of ordinary travelers.
- Recovered Darik Venn's hidden ward-iron proof and helped push Darik toward accountability.
- Gained Garron Flint's respect enough to receive a ward-iron shield boss upgrade.
- Reached Fighter 4 Champion and chose Crusher +1 Strength.
- Began the Stonewake road journey with Dessa Marn, Jory, and Rook.
- Rescued an injured driver, captured cleanup thieves, recovered stolen cargo at the old tollhouse, and preserved a green-lantern buyer lead toward Stonewake.

## Resulting Canon Shift
Crowstep is aftermath, proof, reward, and emotional foundation. Stonewake Market is now the next major setting. Do not keep stretching Crowstep Crest Ledger as if Brannok has not already smashed the local operation.

## Active Player-Facing Direction
Make the next likely path clear without forcing it:

1. **Finish the wayhouse rest.** Brannok can wake, eat, hear guard/healer updates, check the recovered cargo/driver, and decide how quickly to move.
2. **Continue to Stonewake Market.** The strongest obvious lead is Stonewake, where stolen ward iron, fake safe-route goods, green-lantern buyers, and enchanted gear leads converge.
3. **Deliver proof properly.** Brannok should find Sergeant Elian Voss first, then go to the Stonewake Ward Office with chain of custody protected.
4. **Meet the planned companion in Stonewake.** Mirelle Thorn should appear through action involving false road-safety charms, stolen ward iron, or predatory market brokers.

The DM may present these directions through Dessa, road guards, the injured driver, Calder/Mara's papers, Garron's advice, and Stonewake arrival pressure. Brannok must choose.

## Planning Gate Rule
Before continuing any new major path, silently identify which planned track the player is taking:

- **Track A: Crowstep aftermath and downtime.** Use only if Brannok returns to Crowstep or asks for unresolved Crowstep follow-up.
- **Track B: Stonewake Market main lead.** Current default. Use Stonewake stolen-ward-iron / fake-road-safety buyer arc and introduce Mirelle Thorn.
- **Track C: East-road/goblin follow-up.** Use Skrit's goblins, road work, local trust, and possible minor road threat.
- **Track D: Garron/Darik follow-up.** Use accountability, repair work, old ward iron, and Brannok's mine-guilt reflection.
- **Track E: Unplanned player direction.** Stop before launching into major content. Create or revise a short DM plan first, then continue.

## Unplanned Path Protocol
If Brannok chooses a path not covered by an existing planned track:

1. Pause out of character briefly.
2. State that this path is not in the current outline.
3. Create a short private plan before continuing. The plan must include:
   - likely location;
   - immediate conflict;
   - 1-2 relevant NPCs only;
   - enemy or obstacle motive;
   - what clue/reward/consequence can result;
   - how it connects back to Crowstep, Stonewake, or Brannok's growth.
4. Then resume play at the next concrete scene.

Do not improvise an entire new arc on the fly without this checkpoint.

## Planned Near-Term Story Beats
Flexible beats, not rails.

### Beat 1: Wayhouse Morning
Purpose: close the travel-night rescue cleanly.
Possible details:
- Brannok wakes in Dessa's wagon with Rook nearby.
- Road guards report tollhouse thieves collected or on their way.
- Injured driver lives and confirms cargo value or gratitude.
- Dessa suggests moving early before green-lantern buyer gets too far ahead.
- If the player asks about XP, award for the north-road rescue/captures/recovery then.

Keep brief unless player lingers.

### Beat 2: Road to Stonewake
Purpose: simple travel completion, not filler.
Options:
- One compact watchfulness scene where Rook spots signs of the side-track cart.
- Evidence of green-lantern route: wheel ruts, dropped green wax, or a paid waymarker.
- Do not add a full extra combat unless player chooses to pursue immediately.

### Beat 3: Stonewake Market Arrival
Purpose: introduce wider stakes and new companion.
First scene should be actionable:
- market dispute involving fake road-safety tokens/charms;
- a broker trying to cheat a caravan widow;
- hired blades trying to seize Mirelle Thorn's ledger case;
- a green-lantern-linked agent spotted near a ward-token stall.

Mirelle Thorn should appear as charismatic, competent, and physically vulnerable enough that Brannok's protective instincts may engage. Do not lore-dump her backstory.

### Beat 4: Stonewake Authority Contact
Purpose: give Brannok a lawful route without making it mandatory.
- Find Sergeant Elian Voss through the note.
- Use Mara's seal-strip to force clean chain of custody at the ward office.
- Let suspicious Stonewake clerks/guards react differently based on what Brannok says and shows.

### Beat 5: Stonewake Conflict
Purpose: expose buyers of stolen ward iron and fake safe-route goods.
Enemy types:
- market broker with hired blades;
- corrupt charm-seller;
- smugglers moving old ward iron;
- caravan fixer using intimidation;
- raiders/beasts exploiting weak ward markers.

Keep motives understandable: money, route control, stolen materials, protection rackets.

## Current Active Hook: Green-Lantern Buyer
The green-lantern buyer/agent fled before being identified. This is not a separate endless chase arc; it should feed Stonewake.

Use clues such as:
- green lantern wax/residue;
- a hooded cart seen near Stonewake's south gate;
- a phrase: "clean wool";
- buyers who ask if goods were searched by watch;
- a market stall or broker using green lanterns as discreet signals.

## Companion Integration: Mirelle Thorn
Use the DM-only companion lore and character sheet. Do not force her into the party.

Initial reason to work together:
- Brannok carries Crowstep proof, road credibility, and hammer authority.
- Mirelle has Stonewake contacts, social access, and knowledge of fake-road-safety fraud.
- Each has something the other lacks.

Party dynamic target:
- Brannok: blunt protection, frontline, practical judgment.
- Mirelle: social leverage, backline support, sharp words, fragile courage, hidden wounds.

## Story Simplicity Guardrails
Every major scene should be explainable in one sentence.
Examples:
- "A merchant is selling fake road charms."
- "A repair crew is missing."
- "A wagon has been marked safe when the road is not."
- "A broker wants the proof destroyed."
- "Raiders are using a weak ward gap."

If a scene needs more than two paragraphs of lore to explain the stakes, simplify before play.

## Player Agency Guardrail
Make the likely next direction obvious, but do not remove choices. Present clear paths such as:
- continue to Stonewake;
- question wayhouse witnesses;
- follow the side track immediately;
- wait for road guards;
- return to Crowstep;
- custom path.

If the player chooses a custom major path, use the Unplanned Path Protocol.
