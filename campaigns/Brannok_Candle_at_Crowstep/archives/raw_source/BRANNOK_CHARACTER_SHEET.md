## Backstory Core
Brannok left the mines after a preventable collapse caused by corrupt owners using cheap supports, bad iron, and falsified safety records. Before he fully understood the truth, he had been used as muscle to keep workers quiet and production moving. He saw warning signs and waited too long to stand up to those above him. When the tunnel came down, he dug people out until his hands split. He saved some, but not enough. His guilt is not stupidity or weakness; it is the belief that if he had stood up sooner, more people might have lived. This drives his hatred of unsafe work, fake safety, polished exploitation, and people who hide danger behind paperwork.

## Roleplay Notes
- Brannok is a tough bastard, not a dumb brute.
- His intelligence is practical and wisdom-based: he reads danger, lies, fear, power, work, and consequences through experience rather than books.
- Hammer/nail humor is bravado and a way to avoid speaking directly about grief.
- He protects frightened civilians, workers, witnesses, and hungry people being used by stronger people.
- He can threaten hard and still show mercy when someone is more fool/tool than true villain.
- Champion growth should feel like better timing, shield work, hammer control, battlefield judgment, and disciplined violence.
- Future Barbarian seed, if chosen later, should feel like protective fury, not mindless stupidity.
