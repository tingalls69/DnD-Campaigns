Use this alongside the original `CLUES_CLOCKS_AND_CONSEQUENCES.md`.

## Simpler Clue Framing
Every major clue should answer one practical question:
- Who lied?
- What road or person is unsafe?
- What was damaged, moved, hidden, or forged?
- Who benefits from delay or panic?
- What can Brannok do about it now?

Avoid clue chains that require understanding abstract magical law.

## Current Clocks
### Crowstep Public Trust
Ticks when evidence is mishandled, rumor spreads, Candle Court is blamed publicly, or prisoners escape.
Failure: riot, scapegoat arrests, or faction violence.

### Road Ward Repair Window
Ticks when the party rests, travels, debates, or fails checks around repair coordination.
Failure: one of the weak routes becomes unsafe before full warning/closure.

### Third Bell Cleanup
Ticks when signals trigger, agents escape, or Marrow's capture becomes known.
Failure: Third Bell destroys evidence, kills witnesses, or relocates remaining supplies.

## Failure Guidance
If Brannok fails a check, do not stop the story. Use:
- injury or HP loss;
- lost time;
- moved evidence;
- enemy alert;
- public blame;
- damaged trust;
- a fleeing suspect;
- choice between saving people and catching culprit.

## Plain-Language Magic Rule
If a road ward does something magical, immediately translate it into:
- This road is unsafe.
- This component is damaged.
- This warning was hidden.
- This supply is needed for repair.
- This person is trying to redirect danger.
- This action buys time but does not fix everything.
