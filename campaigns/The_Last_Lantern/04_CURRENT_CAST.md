# 04 Current Cast

## Protagonists

### Brannok Ashhelm
Blunt dwarf fighter/barbarian. Hammer and shield. Practical, morally serious, funny in a rough way, not stupid. Carries guilt from a preventable mine collapse where he saw signs and waited too long. His rage is disciplined protective fury.

### Selene Vaer
Charismatic warlock, dramatic, wounded, sharp, and deeply tied to chosen family. Her story is identity, consequence, witnesses, memory, and refusing to be owned or erased. She can be terrifying, hilarious, vulnerable, and kind in the same scene.

## Brannok Core Allies
- Rook: soot-black crow, cannot speak words, rude, clever, bread thief, morale hazard.
- Nera Cale: Carter’s Rest innkeeper, feeds people like she is threatening them, practical refuge anchor.
- Mirelle Thorn: half-elf Lore Bard / ledger ally, social support, fragile courage, not a frontliner.
- Sergeant Elian Voss: male Ward sergeant, lawful, tired, practical, respects results.
- Kaelis Vael: survivor and bow-capable ally; must remain a person with agency, not “the rescued one.”
- Pellan Orsik: dwarf smith, blunt and honest, road metal expert.
- Tovin Brack: half-dwarf wardwright, practical fear and technical courage.
- Garrow: old road-binder, useful and compromised, accountability required.

## Selene Core Allies
- Ves: cat-sized pseudo-dragon familiar with catlike personality, violet magic, nonverbal, proud, possessive.
- Marcella Viretti: social blade, romantic partner, elegant, sharp, vulnerability hidden in style.
- Kara: protector, faith, mercy with backbone, frontline shield.
- Eya: survivor care, memory, gentle truth, emotional stabilizer.
- Jasna: legal/evidence knife, records, truth made official.
- Zula Ozu: wardcraft, tattoos, practical magic, counter-magic, dangerous curiosity with a conscience.
- Sewing Needle / Nessa Vale: tabaxi scout/rogue, barefoot, dark/mysterious, architecture menace, deflects care.
- Mara: fighter/bodyguard, blunt wall, “the door,” protective and not soft about it.

## New / Rebuilt Crossover NPCs

### Commander Rhea Varric
Leader of the Hearthline refugee crossing. Ex-soldier turned evacuation commander. Tired, funny when cornered, brutally practical, no patience for heroes making speeches while wagons are stuck.

### Orvaine Thresh
The institutional villain. Not a god. Not a concept. A person who believes civilization requires choosing acceptable losses. Calm, patient, horrifyingly reasonable, and personally responsible for routing harm.

### The Hearthline Children
Not plot devices. A recurring chorus of fear, jokes, bad questions, Rook fandom, Ves worship, and reminders that the road matters because people live on it.
