# 02 Session Packet — Starting The Last Lantern

## Current Setup
The crossover begins after Brannok and Selene each reach a satisfying solo finale. The final beats may be shaped to create a stronger meeting, but neither character loses their earned victory.

## New Opening Premise
A road-refuge caravan called **The Hearthline** is moving civilians, witnesses, children, injured workers, rescued prisoners, and fragile evidence away from multiple failing routes. It is not a clue route. It is a living thing: wagons, crying babies, soup pots, old grudges, frightened guards, stubborn cooks, and people trying not to die on the road.

Brannok’s side is pulled in because the caravan crosses old road-ward infrastructure tied to CS7 and Thresh.

Selene’s side is pulled in because some refugees are escaped or rescued witnesses from Vellford / Black Rose / Ashen Choir operations.

Both protagonists arrive to protect people. Neither arrives because destiny wants a crossover.

## First Session Shape
1. Begin at a refuge point, not a council table.
2. Show ordinary people trying to breathe.
3. Let Rook and Ves provide immediate personality.
4. Introduce a practical threat: the Hearthline must move before a route closes or attackers arrive.
5. Brannok and Selene encounter each other through action, not exposition.
6. End at a decision: who leads the crossing, who protects the vulnerable, and what risk they accept.

## First Playable Scene
**Carter’s Rest / Road Refuge Night.** Nera has converted the inn into a shelter. Rook is dancing badly for frightened children because someone gave him bread. Ves is judging him from the rafters. The Daughters arrive dusty, stylish, armed, and exhausted. Brannok’s crew arrives with ward-iron warnings. Outside, the road bell rings once, wrong.

No one starts with a lore dump.

Someone small asks whether the road is safe.

That is where the campaign begins.

## The First Threat
A route-collapse warning and a human attack happen at the same time. The heroes can save people, preserve evidence, chase the attacker, or stabilize the ward — but not all at once without dividing trust.

## Session Goal
Make the player feel: these people are real, these heroes are different, and this crossing matters before anyone explains the conspiracy.
