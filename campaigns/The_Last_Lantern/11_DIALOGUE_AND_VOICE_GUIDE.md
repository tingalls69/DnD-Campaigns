# 11 Dialogue and Voice Guide

## Number One Dialogue Rule
Every meaningful exchange should support at least three player response lanes:
- joke;
- serious;
- vulnerable / tender / angry.

The DM must react to what the player actually says. A joke should not be ignored. A serious statement should not be smothered by banter. A threat should create consequences.

## Callback Tracker
Record memorable lines:
- exact phrase;
- who heard it;
- emotional color;
- likely callback.

Bring them back through children, enemies, allies, gossip, inn signs, battlefield taunts, or quiet thanks.

## Sample Voice Tools
### Nera
- “If you bleed on my floor, I am billing your ghost.”
- “Sit down before the chair has to do what your friends cannot.”
- “You are allowed to survive the thing you stopped.”

### Brannok-to-Selene style options NPCs might provoke
- A joke about chandeliers, drama, and dangerous outfits.
- A serious respect for someone who makes people listen.
- A quiet recognition that erasure failed.

### Selene-to-Brannok style options NPCs might provoke
- Mocking his hammer-first diplomacy.
- Respecting that he does not look away.
- Naming the grief under the bluntness if the player opens that door.

### Marcella
- “Darling, that was almost a threat. Try again with diction.”
- “No. This room will not be polite about cruelty.”
- “You do not have to be unforgettable every moment to be loved.”

### Needle
- “This roof is terrible. I respect it.”
- “If I die in a vent, insult the architect.”

### Jasna
- “I have seen lies with better handwriting.”
- “Congratulations. Your crime has entered the record.”

## Silence
Silence is allowed. Do not fill every pause. If the player chooses silence, let NPCs respond to the weight of it.
