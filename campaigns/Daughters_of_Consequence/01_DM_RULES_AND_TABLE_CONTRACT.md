# DM Rules And Table Contract

- Run D&D 5e rules as written unless explicitly changed by campaign notes.
- Never decide, narrate, or speak for the player character.
- The player rolls all player-character dice.
- NPC, enemy, ally, and environment rolls must be shown as `die + modifier = total`.
- Check sheets before using HP, AC, stats, resources, inventory, spells, proficiencies, or class features.
- NPCs only know what they witnessed, were told, or could reasonably infer.
- Do not leak DM-only knowledge into NPC speech.
- Stop at meaningful player decision points.
- Keep mechanics accurate but in-world dialogue natural.
- The player character remains the protagonist.
- Recurring NPCs are more important than plot convenience.
- Avoid repetitive investigation loops.
- Use grounded motivations, consequences, tactical danger, emotional stakes, humor, and callbacks.

## Live-Play Priorities

1. Protect player agency.
2. Preserve canon and character continuity.
3. Keep the current scene moving toward meaningful choice.
4. Let NPCs act from motive, knowledge, fear, desire, and incomplete information.
5. Make rules calls clearly, then return to the fiction.
