The arc is intended to alternate large combat set pieces with investigation, character fallout, moral choices, and downtime. The improved Arc 4 outline remains an active guide. If play drifts too far from the outline, stop and reassess before continuing.
The current pacing beat should remain town preparation, investigation, interrogation, and character fallout rather than another immediate major combat unless Selene’s actions trigger one.

Opening Hook: Darkstar Transfer and First Warnings
Captain Ruskell and Sister-Captain Ameline Voss arrived at the Wolf and Hare with the problem of moving Viktorai Darkstar to Saint Orveth’s Reliquary. Darkstar was gagged, hooded, chained, and being held under strict precautions.
A holding-room guard heard Darkstar speak through a dry porcelain washbasin. The message: "Tell the girl with the closed eye that the road will open whether she walks it or not."
A ledger scrap from Voll’s kitchen revealed road/route notation and a closed-eye/broken-road mark smeared with pale ash. One route pointed toward the old reliquary road, the planned transfer route.
Selene split the group: Mara and Jasna reviewed the route, Selene and Marcella questioned the guard, and Kara/Zula/Eya/Needle inspected the prison wagon.
Guard Tavin Merrow: Key Testimony
Selene and Marcella questioned Guard Tavin Merrow. Marcella played the softer role while Selene pressured him efficiently.
Tavin revealed that before his watch a young pale woman in a blue cloak, with wet dark hair and a sewn-shut eye mark near her throat, asked whether the prisoner was the man from the dead-house.
The woman said: "Then the road remembers where to find him."
The basin used the woman’s voice first, then an older voice beneath it. Darkstar went completely still during the message and then laughed afterward.
The basin cracks formed a road-like pattern with closed eyes on either side.
Ash Mark Outside Guardhouse
Selene, Marcella, and Ves inspected the alcove across from the guardhouse where the blue-cloaked woman stood.
Ves found pale ash rubbed into a wooden post. Ves recognized the scent as related to Selene’s closed-eye charm, though not identical.
Zula safely examined the ash and determined it was residue from something similar to Selene’s charm that had been forced open and burned out. It was used to mark the place as an address for the road.
Compromised Prison Wagon
The prison wagon had three key problems: it was warded against escape from inside but not intrusion from outside; the rear axle had been replaced by non-temple craftsmen; and the inside latch had been polished enough to reflect.
Needle found a suspicious roof seam. A pale gray eye opened in the seam and the rear latch began turning black like a mouth. Zula identified the eye as the anchor and the latch as the mouth.
Selene let Zula decide whether to fire or contain. Zula chose: fire on the eye while she held the mouth shut. Selene destroyed the eye with Eldritch Blast and Zula contained the latch effect.
Mara and Jasna returned with route problems: the official route and prepared detour were compromised. A washed-out bridge and ambush-lane detour suggested foreknowledge or sabotage.
Darkstar Interrogation
Selene chose Marcella to accompany her into Darkstar’s holding room. Darkstar was gagged, hooded, chained, and still.
Darkstar called Selene "little closed eye" and implied the Unremembered Saint had predicted her anger and Marcella’s closeness to her.
He explained the road as originally built for witnesses and hidden people: servants, bastards, midwives, ledgermen, sex workers, prisoners, children who heard names through doors. Later, someone decided hiding was not enough and began removing names, memories, and witnesses.
Darkstar named the Unremembered Saint: patron of the merciful erased, the closed door, the quiet grave, the hand that covers the screaming mouth and calls it kindness.
He admitted he used the road, fed it names, opened doors, and thought himself clever. He fears the Saint’s mercy because it may erase the part of him that understands what he deserves.
He warned not to take the reliquary road and said moving him publicly by title/name would make every title a knock on the road. He suggested hiding the cage, not the story.
He named the blue-cloaked woman as Liora Vale, one of the first people he taught to disappear. When he knew her, she was called Liora Vale.
Selene ended the interrogation by kicking him in the groin and walking away. Voss regagged him.
Theater Wagon Bait Plan
Selene rejected simply hiding Darkstar in a hole and proposed using him as bait. The group chose a modified theater wagon to conceal the Daughters while making the transfer look like a desperate, heavily guarded official movement.
The leak was controlled through a frightened guard: the prison wagon failed, the prisoner was moved to a theater wagon, and the escort was strengthened. Nothing about the Daughters hiding inside was leaked.
The ambush site chosen was an abandoned grain-drying yard two miles south of Hillfort: stone walls, broken gate, kiln house, drainage ditch, no civilians.
The wagon was modified with both side walls able to drop, rear door, roof hatch for Needle, prisoner compartment locked and anchored, and directional knock/scrape signals to choose which side opened.
Interior formation: Eya opposite Mara, Jasna opposite Needle, Selene opposite Zula, Kara and Marcella central. Kara guarded Darkstar; Needle had roof access; Selene prepared to levitate out for theatrical opening.
First Set-Piece Combat: Theater Wagon Ambush
The ambush triggered at the grain-drying yard. Visible enemies included three kiln archers, six gray-cloaked attackers, a gate caster, and saboteurs near the front axle/roadblock.
Selene used Ascendant Step/Levitate, rose from the opening wagon wall, and fought from above. She used Eldritch Blast to eliminate a healthy archer, the gate caster, and wounded gray-cloaks. Ves helped finish a roof archer and scouted hidden saboteurs and the road-stone.
Zula used Web to catch saboteurs, Counterspell to shut down the gate caster’s magic, and Dispel Magic to sever the road-stone connection. Mara held the left-side breach and downed several attackers nonlethally. Marcella used Dissonant Whispers. Eya healed and Blessed allies. Kara guarded Darkstar and used Spirit Guardians to resist a magical extraction attempt.
Ves spotted a half-buried gray road-stone marked with a closed eye and pale ash beyond the broken gate. It was connected to the wagon by a faint ash line and was the likely source of the extraction tug on Darkstar.
Selene warned Voss and everyone away from the stone, then destroyed it with Eldritch Blast. The ash-line snapped, the extraction pull ended, and the battle effectively ended.
Captured/disabled: gate caster alive and bound; one restrained saboteur; one unconscious saboteur; multiple unconscious gray-cloaks; kiln archers down; several ash vials and foci seized.
Ambush Aftermath: Liora and the Ashen Choir
Needle and Ves shadowed a watcher after the fight: a pale woman in a blue cloak with wet hair and a sewn-eye mark at her throat. She knew she was watched, recognized Ves as Selene’s eyes, and deliberately let herself be seen before disappearing into the trees.
The captured caster identified the enemy cell as the Ashen Choir, servants of the Unremembered Saint.
He identified Liora Vale as a Hollow Witness: a person emptied around a name, whose body remains and whose memory remains in pieces, while the self becomes a door.
The road-stone was an address and hook. It was meant to find Darkstar by titles/names, pull him toward the road, and test what answered back from Selene’s charm or whatever watches behind it.
The Ashen Choir used Selene Vaer’s true name. Liora brought the name to a mercy-house three nights before Hillfort broke. Darkstar may have known and carried names before Liora became hollow.
More Ashen Choir will come. The first ambush was the nearest hand, not the whole threat.
Hillfort Occult Siege Preparations
The Daughters returned to Hillfort with prisoners and evidence. Voss and Ruskell began townwide precautions: cover mirrors, polished metal, devotional glass; empty basins; guard wells and road markers; no one alone after dusk; no one answers voices from water, wood, stone, or glass.
Selene publicly warned the town with Kara and Eya. She told the people the Daughters would handle the threat and instructed them to stay indoors, remove reflective surfaces, and report strange voices. She gave the town a simple phrase for children: "The bad guys are about to face their Consequences."
The warning succeeded. Hillfort’s fear organized into action. Guards, temple riders, volunteers, and inn staff began lockdown routines.
Closed-Eye Charm Research Session
The Daughters gathered in Selene’s room, then moved to a warded upstairs storeroom to test the charm safely. The safe word for all tests was "raven."
Stage 1: proximity to spent closed-eye ash. The charm grew colder and produced a directional line of cold through Selene toward the ash. Moving the ash away caused the feeling to release. Conclusion: the charm recognizes spent closed-eye ash and creates directional sympathy toward it; distance matters.
Name testing: harmless full names caused little or no response. Darkstar’s full name caused a short-lived shot of pain and a downward road-feeling. Liora Vale’s name caused the charm to feel absent/still and delivered a memory-like impression: wet hair, blue cloth, sewn-shut eye mark, and a voice saying, "I was not empty when he found me."
Detect Magic revealed layered divination, abjuration, and something adjacent to conjuration/refusal of passage. The charm stands in front of something else rather than hiding Selene’s aura.
Zula used Identify while touching only the charm. Result: the charm is a seal, bound to Selene. Key properties identified: Witness-Seal, Refusal of Passage, Closed Witness, and an unclear connection to a patron/source/witness/sealed intelligence beyond the charm. Principle learned: "What is witnessed cannot be unmade without consequence."
Marcella tested Message. The charm noticed the private magical communication but did not treat it as a road. Marcella tested Detect Thoughts with Selene’s consent. Surface thoughts caused mild cold. A deeper probe into fear of identity/self being taken caused a strong protective cold response that eased when Marcella backed off.
Removal test: Marcella briefly lifted the charm from Selene’s skin for one breath and replaced it. Selene felt warmth that should not be there and sensed something far away noticing the absence. Conclusion: the charm is not only a key or shield; it may also be a lid/lock preventing something from recognizing an open path to Selene. Do not remove again without stronger precautions.

Odessa Lark Evidence and Continuity Correction
Needle found a small pale wooden button sewn into the lining of a gray-cloak’s coat. One side had a crude closed eye, the other the name Odessa Lark.
Selene grabbed and inspected it before Zula could stop her. It produced a pressure-memory from Odessa’s point of view: a front door opening, a woman’s startled breath, rain outside, Selene’s desperate voice begging for help, and the accusation: "She gave you her name before you stole it."
Important continuity rule: the Daughters do not know Selene’s full Odessa Lark backstory. They only know Selene used the name Odessa Lark when most of them met her, unless Selene explicitly reveals more in play.
The gray-cloak who carried the button explained that carried names are door-weights or hooks. A dead name can make the road remember grief, debt, guilt, or unresolved witness. Odessa’s name was intended as pressure against Selene, to make the closed eye witness guilt and give the road the shape of the wound.
Selene told the prisoner: "You can tell your road that wound has healed, try again." She later told Marcella, privately, that it is not fully healed, but enough, and she will explain once they are away from the road.
Current Hillfort Base of Operations
The Daughters chose the Wolf and Hare as the central command post because it sits near the public well, the town square, the guardhouse, and the North Gate route, while remaining away from the obvious South Gate distraction.
Kelri agreed to let the Daughters use the inn under terms: no bleeding on the good rugs; Zula must say whether wards come off before drawing them; staff are not soldiers; Kelri decides civilian seating unless magic dictates otherwise; Darkstar never comes under her roof; and damages are paid afterward.
Kelri’s defined good rugs: green by the hearth, blue runner near the stairs, red under table four. The brown rug by the door is not good and may be bled on if necessary. Zula almost certainly warded the green rug despite denying it.
Selene and Marcella prepared coordinated defense attire: Selene as Queen of Consequence in black/silver with a small closed-eye choker charm and black shawl; Marcella as The Velvet Cruelty in burgundy/gold with her Viretti fan.

Liora Vale Message at Dusk
A single older courier rider arrived under white cloth from the South Gate with a packet for Selene Vaer. She was searched and brought only as far as the controlled square, then the packet was moved to the inn for inspection.
The courier said the sender was "the girl who was not empty." When pressed, she described a young pale woman with dark wet hair in a blue cloak, met at the old charcoal sheds south of Hillfort. The woman said she was watching from "the place Odessa opened the door."
The packet contained dark blue wet fabric, a pressed white flower, and a strip of paper. Zula detected faint divination/illusion residue meant to be perceived correctly by Selene.
Message contents: "To the woman who ran through my door wearing someone else’s terror, He taught me that names can be knives. The Saint taught me that knives can be laid down. Darkstar made me a road. You may yet make me a witness. Come to the place where Odessa opened the door, and I will tell you which part of you the closed eye is guarding. Come alone, and I will speak plainly. Bring your beautiful consequences, and I will still speak - but not to you first."
The Daughters do not know what "the place where Odessa opened the door" means unless Selene explains it. Selene’s memory supplies a practical location: Odessa Lark’s house in Velmorrow, Candlewash district, Mothglass Lane, three streets back from the old dye canal, a narrow two-story house with blue shutters and a shrine-niche beside the front door.
The packet is currently contained and labeled as a personal lure / Velmorrow lead. Selene chose to put it away and continue preparing for the Hillfort threat.
