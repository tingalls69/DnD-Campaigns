# Arc 4 Running Log — v4 Addendum

This file appends the latest play to the prior v3 running log. Earlier events remain archived in the previous files.

## Rusk Warehouse
The Daughters returned from sanctuary rest and pursued Halven Rusk to a dry goods warehouse near the North Market. Selene led the canal-side Teeth Team while the authority front drew eyes. The copper-seamed safe had a burn charm, which Zula dispelled. Jasna opened the safe under fire. Rusk tried to lower a satchel of papers from the upstairs hoist, but Zula cut the rope with Fire Bolt and secured the satchel. Selene used Dimension Door with Mara to reach Rusk upstairs; Mara grappled him alive. Ves stung Rusk but he resisted the poison. The warehouse was secured, and the records revealed the Path of Consequence: buyer clusters, towns, estates, and a nearest urgent lead at Marrowmere Hunting Lodge.

## Path of Consequence Named
Selene named the expanding pursuit **the Path of Consequence**. It began as a joke with Daughters branding, matching knives, initials, roses, and shawls, but became the working case title. Jasna wrote it as a case heading. The path currently includes Marrowmere, Grey Lantern Road, Vellford, Northmere, Ashcombe House, Crowmere Crossing, and the unknown silver-capped-cane receiver.

## Marrowmere Hunting Lodge
The Daughters split into three teams: front, carriage, and cellar. The front team confronted Corven Rill; the carriage team secured the green-latched chest; the cellar team freed captives. Selene found and freed Ranna Wren, a hidden witness held in a cupboard under the stairs. Ranna was a washerwoman/laundry runner near Odessa Lark's home years ago. She saw the frightened girl fleeing, saw Odessa help, and lied to Aurelian's hunters to buy the girl time. She did not know adult Selene by face; she pieced it together through Selene's name, reaction, and the old context. Ranna is not Ranna Vale; the corrected name is Ranna Wren.

The cook was revealed as a coerced collaborator who fed prisoners and held keys but also looked away and sometimes administered laudanum. Selene refused to forgive her and told her to help while she still could. Varric ordered the cook held as witness/collaborator, not free and not yet condemned.

The green-latched chest contained Marrowmere private records, servant rosters, payment strips, red-pinned names, and evidence tying Corven Rill and House Marrowmere to the network.

## Garron Pike, the Antler-Knife
After returning to Saint Orveth's, the Daughters learned that Marrowmere's field enforcer, Garron Pike, called the Antler-Knife, was unaccounted for. He was a private huntsman responsible for recovering escaped servants, training hounds, escorting illegal transfers, handling difficult cargo, and maintaining the lower trophy hall/root cellar.

Selene requested a short rest before pursuing Pike. Selene spent 2 Hit Dice, healing 10 HP and reaching 57/65. Pact slots refreshed. Zula and Eya remained behind due to wounds/survivor care; Jasna stayed to map evidence. Strike team: Selene, Ves, Needle, Mara, Kara, Marcella.

At the gamekeeper's cottage, Needle spotted traps and Pike's prepared ambush. Ves scouted up and found Pike on a hunting platform with a scarred hunting beast below. Ves stung Pike twice; the second sting caused Pike to fail by 5+ and fall unconscious. Selene used Hold Monster on the beast to save Kara. Mara and Kara punished it while paralyzed, it later broke free, and Selene finally killed it with Eldritch Blast. Pike was bound, disarmed, stripped of his black iron whistle, mask, weapons, hidden blades, and collar knife.

A chained, muzzled captive in the shed was revealed not to be a monster but **Bren Calder**, a huge woodsman from Grey Lantern Road. Pike had muzzled, chained, and kept him near the beast after Bren refused to track escaped people and helped one girl find the wrong trail to safety. Bren is not altered. Pike tried to make him feel like a beast; mechanically and narratively Bren is a large traumatized human woodsman.

## Root Cellar
The cottage root cellar contained no living prisoners but held trophies, personal effects, trail maps, waxed ledgers, a black iron brand, manacles, and bodies. The party took minimal evidence and left the room sealed for Varric, Jasna, and proper witnesses. Bren can help name some trails later.

## Return and Pike Interrogation
Back at Saint Orveth's, Bren was placed in a private east infirmary room, away from the survivor chapel, with door open and guard outside. Eya began actual 5e healing and treatment. Pike was secured separately.

Selene asked Marcella to lead Pike's interrogation. Marcella used social control instead of threats. Pike revealed Grey Lantern Road's start: southwest of the gamekeeper's cottage, behind the split pine, dead oak with three iron nails, then stone culvert under ridge road with a grey lantern mark. He identified a grey-hooded, tall, thin receiver with a silver-capped cane but withheld the true identity out of fear.

Pike revealed seven living captives in the last month: Bren Calder; a girl using the name Nessa Vale from Vellford estate-service records; Father Ell; two Northmere boys, Tovin and maybe Renn; a serving woman from Ashcombe House returned for remembering too much; and a mute man from the old quarry road. Needle's old name is Nessa Vale; the girl Pike caught was not Needle, but someone else wearing/assigned that name.

Selene ordered that Pike never be allowed to think he is safe. One Daughter must be present for custody at all times.

# v6 Running Log Addendum — Grey Lantern Operation and Level 10

After resting and planning at Saint Orveth's, the Daughters executed the Grey Lantern operation. Pike's information proved true: the culvert was a decoy and the actual handoff was at the dry ford half a mile south. The operation prioritized protection first, capture second. Selene began with the dry-ford protection team while Needle, Mara, and Ves formed the capture net.

At the dry ford, Selene signaled Ves to mark the silver-capped receiver, then blasted Handler 1 away from the oilskin tubes before he could grab them. Kara and the guards formed the shield line. Zula killed a burn charm, and Jasna secured the first tube. The receiver began withdrawing toward the escape path. Needle and Mara engaged the capture side, but Needle was badly wounded by the receiver scout. Ves signaled urgent danger through the bond. Selene asked Zula whether she should come; Zula chose to stay because if she left the names might burn. Selene used Dimension Door to join the capture team.

The silver-capped receiver used his cane to bind Selene with silver lines after the black glass bead in the cane cap caught her reflection. This restrained Selene and blocked Dimension Door while active. Ves then successfully stung the receiver; he failed the save badly enough to fall unconscious. The binding broke. Selene helped drop the scout with Eldritch Blast while Mara held him. Needle secured the receiver and cane.

Selene dashed back to the dry ford. Eya discovered a child concealed in a covered mule sling. Zula neutralized the final hidden burn-thread on the last tube, Jasna secured the remaining oilskin tubes, and Selene used Levitate to gain a higher view and call out the hidden lookout's location. Guards captured the lookout. Handler 2 was manacled by Kara; Handler 3 was contained; Handler 1 stayed down. The dry ford was secured.

The child was revealed as **Miri Tallow**. Selene reassured Miri that she was not in trouble, told her she could say her name as much as she wanted, introduced Ves, and later walked Miri into a recovery room at Saint Orveth's. Miri asked for Ves, and Ves stayed with her for a short visit that became a protective footboard watch.

The Daughters opened the three oilskin tubes in the records room. Recovered names included Miri Tallow, Bren Calder, Ranna Wren, Odessa Lark, Selene Vaer, Tovin Brant, Renn Brant, Lysa Cale, Nessa Vale, and Father Ell. Tube notes showed Odessa as dead witness/residual use prohibited, Ranna as held witness/not to be released, Selene as not service-ready with recognition risk and silver-cane verification pending, and Nessa Vale as an active duplicate/stolen-name use. The final buyer mark was a black rose inside a square. The silver-capped receiver is listed as Cane-Signatory.

After night work, Eya healed Needle and Zula using tracked 5e resources, Jasna copied and sealed the names, Zula secured the cane, and Varric locked down prisoner custody. Selene slept and experienced a patron dream through the closed-eye charm: names refusing erasure, with the phrase **What is witnessed cannot be unmade without consequence.** Selene gained Level 10 and the patron feature Witness That Cannot Be Erased.

XP after Grey Lantern used catch-up distribution: Selene/Kara/Jasna/Zula/Needle +1,000 and Level 10; Eya/Marcella +1,500 and remain Level 9; Mara +2,500 and remains Level 7. Current save is morning after long rest, with Jasna asking Selene to come to the records room because it concerns Selene's name.

## Larkspur / Ashcombe / Vellford Addendum

The Daughters took their Larkspur shopping day and turned it into an evidence-and-rescue operation. In Larkspur Crossing, they found the black rose-square service-bond network, confronted the auction clerk, and rescued Lia Fen, Marin Bell, and Tessa Grain from a false-name market transfer. The records pointed to Vellford and to the old Black Rose / Domestic Benevolence structure. The Daughters split into a rescue team and a records team. The important current result is simple: the victims are safe, the evidence is secure, and the next target is Vellford itself. The party returned to Saint Orveth’s with the prisoners, witness children, route slips, transfer index, black rose-square fragments, and the magistrate’s threat note. The Daughters agreed to go at dawn for Vellford.
