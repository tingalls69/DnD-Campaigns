# DM Review, Pacing, and Design Notes — v4

## What Is Working
- Selene remains the protagonist while allies act competently.
- The Daughters ensemble has clear roles.
- Concrete rescue/action/evidence beats are much stronger than abstract ritual logic.
- Villains with visual identity and table function work: Mother Ash, Corven Rill, Garron Pike.
- Humor under pressure keeps the campaign from becoming misery sludge.
- Emotional beats land when they are not stacked too tightly.

## What To Improve
- Avoid therapy queues. After heavy emotional scenes, move to logistics, shopping, planning, or action.
- Avoid repeated "go to place, someone burns evidence/runs" structures.
- Avoid mook-only fights; meaningful enemies need durability, terrain, and tactical goals.
- Avoid overloaded names that sound similar. Do not add more Vale/Vaer/Vane-style confusion.
- Avoid using Elian Voss in Daughters; he is Crowstep-only.

## Combat Difficulty
Use Hard as the baseline for serious combats with multiple Daughters. Use Deadly only for bosses/finales. Death should be rare but possible. Enemies should not routinely die in one hit unless they are true mooks.

## Rewards
Add tangible progression:
- functional loot from villains;
- shopping/prep day;
- upgraded tools/armor/weapons/components;
- modest magic or semi-magic items with narrow functions.

## Skill Check Discipline
For non-contested tasks, set the fixed DC before the roll. Do not retroactively decide based on the result. Use no roll for trivial/routine actions or impossible actions.

## Mechanics Discipline
Before combat, confirm:
- allies present;
- HP/AC if likely relevant;
- spell slots/resources;
- active spells/prepared spell lists;
- enemy objectives;
- civilian/evidence stakes.


# v5 Pacing Note

The Marrowmere/Pike sequence delivered the requested stronger mini-boss beat: Pike had a visual identity, prepared terrain, traps, a dangerous beast, a captive, and a root cellar consequence. Continue this approach for major combats: memorable enemy, tactical objective, real danger, and concrete stakes.

The immediate next beat should not be another heavy trauma conversation unless the user initiates it. Move toward rest, prep, loot/resource clarity, and the Grey Lantern tactical operation.


## Finale Rush

The campaign is now in the last sprint. Keep each scene short enough to matter and concrete enough to resolve. The current pattern should be: identify the objective, enter the place, rescue the victims, seize the records, capture the villain, move on. Avoid any return to repetitive investigation loops, endless shopping, or multi-scene metaphysical explanations.

After Vellford, close the Daughters’ finale cleanly and then use the crossover materials for **The Iron Road** only as DM-only guidance. Do not merge the two campaigns early.
