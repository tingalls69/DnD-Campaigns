Complete Chronological Story Log
Deep callback reference for Selene Vaer and the Daughters of Consequence
Selene's background
Selene Vaer was born from a secret that powerful people tried to erase. She is a half-elf who appears almost entirely human: pale skin, long black hair, violet eyes, black winged eyeliner, black lipstick, black nails, piercings, and a black-and-silver style balanced between sensual elegance and danger. Her mother was Mara Vell, a human prostitute. Her father was eventually identified as Aurelian Tavaril, an elf of wealth and importance connected to House Tavaril. When Aurelian or his house discovered that Selene existed, Mara was killed and Selene was targeted. Selene survived and grew into a social chameleon who uses aliases, documents, disguises, and performance to survive. Her morality is fluid and situational, but she is not heartless. She tends not to prey on people who cannot afford to lose. She uses humor in inappropriate moments, and if she likes someone, she can let that person's needs become too important, which makes intimacy dangerous to her.
Odessa Lark and the stolen life
Selene's central early alias was Odessa Lark, a real woman. Selene, fleeing dangerous agents, hid in Odessa's home and begged Odessa to cover for her. Odessa helped Selene and was killed by the men pursuing Selene. Selene then assumed Odessa's identity and even met Odessa's parents while disguised as her. Selene carries deep guilt over this because Odessa died after helping her. Odessa's invitation later brought Selene to Ibrido and the Castel di Maschera masquerade. Selene often frames this as Odessa's choice of mask and invitation, but the guilt remains Selene's.
The Argent Glass, the charm, and Ves before the masquerade
Before the campaign began, Selene stole a small silver hand mirror because underground whispers suggested powerful people wanted it. She did not know what it did. The item later became known as the Argent Glass and proved tied to blood, identity, debt, reflection, the Shadowfell, and the Witness Behind Glass. Selene's pact with a Great Old One manifested through whispers, dreams, reflections, and truths she did not understand. Her Pact of the Chain familiar, Vesper or Ves, appeared after her first experience of the deep whisper. Ves is an all-black pseudodragon with violet eyes and an opalescent purple sheen, catlike, proud, possessive, and fiercely loyal to Selene.
A Night of Masks and Monsters: arrival at the masquerade
Selene entered Ibrido as Odessa Lark because Odessa had been invited to the Castel di Maschera masquerade. She wore an otter mask, which she later pointed out was Odessa's choice, not hers. The masquerade was beautiful, decadent, and dangerous. Selene avoided Prospero's wine by using tinted water as a prop. Important figures included Kara of the Aureum, Eya Harisa, Jasna, Zula Ozu, Marcella Viretti in a swan mask, Sewing Needle the tabaxi door attendant, Polpo Torabek, and Barba Torabek, who had gone missing after a prior party.
The cursed masks and Akro/Prospero
The party discovered that the masks and wine were part of a curse. Selene investigated Prospero's private chamber while invisible, found a hidden laboratory behind a chimera tapestry, discovered altered bodies including Barba Torabek, and stole Prospero's notebook. Prospero was revealed as Akro Dimiryan, a fiendish or cambion-like host using transformation and identity magic tied to the two-mask symbol. Selene warned allies telepathically and helped trigger the fight. Akro and the giant vulture Malinov were defeated. The masquerade survivors became the first seeds of the group that would become the Daughters.
The first Selene and Marcella memory
At the masquerade, Marcella noticed Selene across the room: too pretty to be harmless, too alert to be decorative, and too alone to be what she claimed. Selene remembered the swan-masked woman assessing her as elegant, beautiful, and absolutely dangerous. A drunk halfling in a dog or corgi mask, Piccolo, also tried to box Selene, a ridiculous memory both later laughed about. Selene fled after the crisis without a goodbye kiss, which became a romantic regret finally answered much later after Hillfort.
Bellwater House and the Open Lid thread
After the masquerade, Selene sheltered at Bellwater House under the identity Elianora Vale. Kara held Prospero's notebook as evidence and sheltered Selene discreetly. Selene rested, mended, studied occult texts, and advanced from Warlock 5 to Warlock 6, gaining Dispel Magic. Kara, Eya, Jasna, and Zula pushed the false-eye/Open Lid investigation forward. The group found references to transformation magic, old Ibridian cult work, and a moth-veiled woman with pale gloves and a silver crescent-hook ear cuff. A message stated that the glass had been seen, the half-blood lived, and the father's house could be named.
Sabine Crowe and Rovino Bell
Selene retired the Elianora identity and became Sabine Crowe, a quiet debt collector/fixer persona. She investigated Rovino Bell, learned he had been paid clean coin and guided toward flood tunnels by the moth-veiled woman, and found him and his sister in a marked room with the false eye scratched into the window. The moth-veiled woman appeared, linked Aurelian's house, the Argent Glass, and Mara's death, named Vael Tavaril, and told Selene to ask Jasna about the white trees. This woman, tracked as C., remains ambiguous, dangerous, manipulative, sometimes helpful, and never a simple ally.
The burial trail and Brindlehook
Rather than rush Vael Tavaril, the group followed Mara Vell's burial trail. Selene remembered the Velvet Lantern, steep stairs, cheap perfume, smoke, rain, and harbor bells. Nella Corvay produced copied records showing Mara had been buried as Mira Vale under a false cause of death. Selene and Eya found Sera Voss living as Sera Vane in Brindlehook. Sera described the gray-gloved elf with pale green ring, white-branch clasp, precise northern speech, and scar beneath the left ear. She gave Selene Mara's package: a lock of Mara's hair, a closed-eye silver charm, and a letter to "my starless girl." Selene wore the charm at her throat, kept Mara's hair safe, memorized the letter, and burned it so the words could not be taken. Mara's letter warned of white trees, long windows, and the pale green ring, and told Selene not to beg her father to love her.
The Daughters of Consequence form
Selene rallied Kara, Eya, Jasna, Zula, Marcella, Sewing Needle, Ves, and eventually Mara into what became the Daughters of Consequence. The name began as banter between Selene and Marcella and became a vow. The Daughters are not passive followers. They are DM-controlled allies with agency, flaws, resources, and personal priorities. Selene remains the only player-controlled character. Kara protects witnesses and heals. Eya cares for victims and testimony. Jasna weaponizes records and contradictions. Zula reads wards, symbols, and magic. Marcella dominates social pressure and later becomes Selene's romantic partner. Needle scouts, steals, binds, and enters through criminal entrances. Ves guards Selene emotionally and tactically. Mara later becomes the blunt fighter/muscle and protective guard.
Vael Tavaril infiltration
The Daughters infiltrated Vael Tavaril. Marcella and Jasna staged a front-gate distraction, with Marcella appearing as an injured noblewoman whose cart had been attacked. Selene, Zula, Sewing Needle, and Ves infiltrated through the service side, while Kara and Eya remained as reserve. Selene cut Marcella's cheek without warning to improve the disguise, creating a real trust wound. The infiltration found servant passages, a private records room, the Births and Claims ledger with a suppressed human-born female claim by order of Lord A. Tavaril, Glassworks Holdings ledgers tied to Aurelian and shell entities, and a black lockbox containing a white-branch letter, pale-green key, and Argent Glass blood confirmation conditions.
The lower root chamber and Aurelian named
Selene re-entered secretly with the Argent Glass, pale-green key, Zula, Needle, Ves, and reserve support. Beneath Vael Tavaril, they found a lower root chamber with iron-bound warded door, pale root, basins, and mirror stand. Selene named witnesses: Kara, Eya, Zula, Sewing Needle, and Vesper. She placed the covered Argent Glass in the frame, cut herself, gave blood to the root, and uncovered the Glass. The Glass showed Mara behind Selene, then Aurelian Tavaril, identifying him as living blood-source, denier, and debtor. The house-bell answered and the group fled.
The cemetery reckoning
The Daughters brought evidence into Ibrido through Aureum, civic, legal, and social channels. House Tavaril tried to frame Selene as a pact-bound criminal claimant. The group chose the canal cemetery as public ground. Aurelian and Caerwyn came. Selene publicly described the root chamber, Glass, blood ritual, and witnesses. When house-binding magic stirred, Selene claimed blood only as proof of debt, rejected any claim over her body or life, and named Aurelian as debtor for discovering her existence, killing Mara, and hunting his own blood. The roots rejected Selene and took Aurelian. The Argent Glass declared debt answered by debtor and claimant unowned. Aurelian vanished beneath the cemetery stone, not confirmed dead. Caerwyn survived, humiliated and hostile.
After the cemetery and bridge to Elfhunt
The Aureum chapterhouse became a defensible shelter. Kara separated evidence and did not press Selene to surrender the Glass, but required it stay covered and away from mirrors. Marcella remained angry about the cheek wound but stayed. Jasna and Valcoran made certified copies and legal records. Zula copied marks. Needle checked exits. Ves watched rafters. The Daughters disagreed on next steps: Kara favored controlled absence, Eya agreed for witness safety, Jasna wanted Selene close enough to testify but far enough from provocation, Zula wanted to follow Open Lid, Marcella told Selene to stop handing enemies the shape of her wounds, and Needle agreed to travel because traps need hinges stolen. Leads involving shell companies, jungle resin, mirror backing, and suspicious retrieval work pointed toward Camp Highmore and Elfhunt.
Elfhunt and Camp Highmore
At Camp Highmore, Imis Elvan had been accused of stealing military patrol information. Selene and the Daughters investigated Filip, Shandra, Johan, Tane's routes, and the camp's message network. Imis had copied unlawful lower-cell transfer records rather than patrol routes. The party used disguise, message theft, Minor Illusion, social pressure, route work, and public contradiction to expose Filip and Shandra. They rescued Johan after his abduction, opened lower cells, found Ariane Vel and other prisoners, used Ren's memory of the dumb bell route, rescued Orren Vale from a purge chamber, uncovered a route chest and lower-cell ledgers, captured a crescent-hook agent, recovered Disposable alive and a partial profit list, and linked Mara/Mira Vale to House Tavaril interests. The arc ended with Highmore forced into daylight, Filip removed, Shandra suspended, Tane as conditional liaison, and multiple witnesses protected.
Level-up and Mara joins
After Camp Highmore, the Daughters received a major XP award and advanced. Selene became Warlock 7 with Dimension Door, Mind Sliver replacing Chill Touch, Investment of the Chain Master, and 4th-level pact slots. Kara became Cleric 7. Eya and Marcella advanced to level 6. Jasna, Zula, and Needle advanced in their own tracks. Mara, the scarred lower-camp woman and former Shandra runner/contact, effectively joined as a fighter/muscle/intimidation role rather than a dagger rogue. She became a direct, protective, volatile sword-and-shield ally.
Hillfort begins
The Daughters arrived in Hillfort after Elfhunt. The town was frightened by disappearances, three-knock patterns, shrine rumors, the old well, and the Hollow Wood. The party stayed at The Wolf and Hare, run by Kelri, who became a blunt and protective ally. Selene roomed with Marcella and transformed into her Queen of Consequence look: black dress, silver jewelry with dark stones, tall black boots, black lace gloves, hair tied up, and the closed-eye charm worn close to the throat like a collar. Kelri identified likely targets: Darra Voss, Noll Tacker, and Sera Halven.
Shrine and Sera rescue
At the shrine, the Daughters discovered an older undercroft with a defaced sunburst, six grooves matching three-knock/pause/three-knock, old bones, newer fragments, and a hidden seam. Undead scouts attacked and were destroyed. Zula marked the route. The party realized Sera Halven was at risk and ran to the shrine house, where undead used Brother Halven's voice to lure her. Kara, Eya, Needle, Mara, Selene, Ves, Zula, Jasna, and Marcella rescued Sera alive, destroyed undead, stopped a fleeing scout before it reached the shrine seam, and marked the shrine house branch.
Darra, Noll, and the branches
The party split into Team Thorns and Team Rose. Selene, Ves, Mara, and Jasna went to Darra Voss at the cooper's shed. Kara, Eya, Zula, Marcella, and Needle went to Noll Tacker at the stable. At Darra's, they proved Harl was taken from below, not through the staged broken window. Selene baited scouts by knocking back; undead emerged under the floor; Ves paralyzed a runner; and the branch was marked. At the stable, Needle found a loose hayloft panel and Zula identified necromancy. Selene blasted undead, Needle stopped a runner, Kara burned undead with Sacred Flame, and Zula marked the stable branch. Noll survived and was vindicated.
Mayor, Perrin, Merren, and the well
Marcella and Jasna confronted Mayor Greaves and council, presenting evidence of four access branches and ignored reports. Dovan Vale, mason and foundation keeper, became the key suspect. A stable fire broke out; Selene, Needle, and Ves chased and captured Perrin Vale, who had set the fire. Selene and Needle then investigated Merren's Copy & Seal, where Merren confessed Dovan coerced her into copying the mayor's seal and documents under threat to Lysa. She gave a target list, forged summons, foundation map fragment, and black-stained thread. Perrin later confirmed Dovan's orders and the phrase "the well opens before the bell." At the old well, the party captured Dovan, destroyed a well-husk, forced Dovan to revoke the invitation, disrupted the grooves, and Selene cut the rope on the rising bucket before its delivery reached the rim. The old well fell silent.
Kalviache, Darkstar, and the house-heart
Dovan named Viktorai Darkstar in the Hollow Wood past the old deer road, split oak, and dead grove. The party entered the Hollow Wood and met Kalviache, an ancient druid separate from Darkstar. Kalviache warned against the watched road, hungry root-way, and memory-way, then opened a hidden living bramble route and told the party to break the house-heart: a black root grown through a burial stone. In Darkstar's ruin, the Daughters found Darkstar, a bound victim, a tall root-husk, rising dead, and the house-heart. Selene attacked Darkstar and the heart. Zula weakened bindings. Needle freed the victim. Darkstar tried to intrude on Selene's mind through reflective magic, but the closed-eye charm helped her resist. Selene cast Dispel Magic on the burial-stone anchor. Ves stung and paralyzed Darkstar. The Daughters broke the burial stone, destroyed the house-heart, bound Darkstar alive, and escaped with Oren Pennic alive as the ruin collapsed.
Hillfort aftermath and Darkstar interrogation
Back in Hillfort, Darkstar, Dovan, Perrin, and later Sergeant Voll were secured. Search teams rescued Mira Dain, then Darkstar's yes-or-no interrogation led to Elska Marr and Jessa Crowth alive near the stable branch behind a necromantic threshold trap. Selene dispelled the trap, Zula and Kara removed the black-thread bindings, and both women survived. A scratched wall list confirmed taken names, interrupted targets, future targets, and the line THE CLOSED EYE - DO NOT TAKE. OPEN. Darkstar revealed that Selene's charm closes intrusion and remembers who tried, while the Argent Glass mirror reaches through the Shadowfell or something using the Shadowfell as a hall to the Witness Behind Glass. He wanted Selene to use the mirror and be opened. He was unrepentant, regretting only failure. Voll was exposed as the last collaborator and the second ledger was recovered.
Selene and Marcella after Hillfort
After Darkstar's interrogation, Selene withdrew to her room, fingering the closed-eye charm. Marcella came to the door and offered company without demanding Selene be inspiring or terrifying. Selene asked her to lay there with her. They spoke about the masquerade, Odessa's otter mask, Piccolo the drunk dog-masked halfling, Marcella's swan mask, and Selene's regret that she left without a goodbye kiss. Marcella kissed her, delayed but not forgotten. Selene called Marcella absolutely astonishing. Their relationship became explicitly romantic and emotionally important, though Marcella remains sharp, elegant, and fully herself.
End of Hillfort
Hillfort survived. Mira, Oren, Elska, and Jessa lived. Harl Voss, Brother Halven, and Tobin Reed were confirmed dead. Remaining bound dead were released. Mayor Greaves admitted failure and funded victim care. Brast was removed from public safety oversight. Noll came under Kelri's protection. Kalviache remained an uneasy ally. Darkstar was transferred under heavy restrictions: gagged, bound, eyes covered near reflections. The Argent Glass was wrapped, boxed, labeled by Marcella and Jasna, and blessed by Kara. The Daughters left with reputation, unresolved mirror/charm/patron questions, and company.
Arc 4: The Road of Closed Eyes begins
After Hillfort, Arc 4 begins with the Daughters at a higher current baseline: Selene 9, Kara 9, Jasna 9, Zula 9, Sewing Needle 9, Eya 8, Marcella 8, Mara 5. Darkstar's transfer to Saint Orveth's Reliquary triggered new warnings. A holding-room guard heard Darkstar speak through a dry porcelain washbasin: "Tell the girl with the closed eye that the road will open whether she walks it or not." A ledger scrap from Voll's kitchen showed road notation and a closed-eye/broken-road mark in pale ash. Guard Tavin Merrow reported a pale young woman in a blue cloak with wet dark hair and a sewn-shut eye mark near her throat saying, "Then the road remembers where to find him." This woman was named by Darkstar as Liora Vale, one of the first people he taught to disappear. Zula identified ash residue like a burned-out cousin of Selene's charm. A prison wagon was compromised from outside by a roof-seam eye and black-mouth latch; Selene and Zula destroyed/contained it. The official route and detour were compromised.
Arc 4: theater wagon ambush and current beat
Selene proposed using Darkstar as bait instead of hiding him. The Daughters chose a modified theater wagon and controlled the leak. The ambush at an abandoned grain-drying yard featured kiln archers, gray-cloaked attackers, a gate caster, saboteurs, a road-stone, and an attempt to extract Darkstar. Selene used Ascendant Step/Levitate, rose from the wagon, and fought from above. Ves helped finish a roof archer and scouted saboteurs. Zula used Web, Counterspell, and Dispel Magic. Kara guarded Darkstar and used Spirit Guardians. Mara held the breach. Needle worked roof and movement. Jasna preserved records and chain of custody. Marcella used bardic/social support. The Daughters captured attackers, destroyed the ash-marked road-stone, learned that Liora Vale is a Hollow Witness tied to Darkstar and the Unremembered Saint, learned that the Ashen Choir has Selene's true name, and returned to Hillfort for lockdown. Current pacing should be town preparation, captured Ashen Choir interrogation, investigation of Selene's true name in ash-road magic, aftermath of the attempted extraction, and character fallout, not immediate repeat combat unless Selene triggers it.
