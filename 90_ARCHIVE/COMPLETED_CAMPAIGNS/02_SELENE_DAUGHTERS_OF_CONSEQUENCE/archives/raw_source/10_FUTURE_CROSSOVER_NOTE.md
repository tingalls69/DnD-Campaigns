# Future Crossover Note - Daughters and Crowstep

The user noticed parallels between Selene Vaer and Brannok Ashhelm and is interested in a future combined campaign.

Do not combine them yet. Each campaign needs its own satisfying finale first.

After both finales, a separate crossover arc could work very well. Selene and Brannok have compatible but distinct wounds:

- Selene carries identity/name/witness trauma and the guilt of surviving while others died because she was hunted.
- Brannok carries responsibility/guilt from waiting too long to act and now refuses to delay when people are in danger.

A crossover should preserve both:
- Selene remains dramatic, dangerous, socially sharp, and surrounded by the Daughters.
- Brannok remains blunt, practical, morally stubborn, road-and-hammer focused, and not stupid.

Potential crossover spine:
A faction uses damaged road wards, false identities, and trade routes to move people, erase witnesses, and control markets. Brannok brings grounded road-ward investigation and hammer justice. Selene brings the Daughters, the Path of Consequence, and witness/name consequences.

Keep any crossover concrete and classic D&D:
- broken wards;
- missing caravans;
- false road-safety documents;
- named villains;
- captives to rescue;
- evidence to seize;
- a final stronghold to clear.

Avoid returning to abstract road/name metaphysics as the core gameplay.
