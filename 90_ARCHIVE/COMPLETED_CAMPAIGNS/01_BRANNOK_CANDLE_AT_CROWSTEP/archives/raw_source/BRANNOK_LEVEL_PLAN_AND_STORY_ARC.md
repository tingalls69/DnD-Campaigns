# Brannok Level Plan and Story Arc

## Mechanical Plan
Current: Fighter 4 / Champion.

Current XP: 2,875 / 6,500.

Chosen Fighter 4 option: Crusher (+1 Strength).
- Strength is now 20.
- Warhammer is +7 to hit and 1d8+7 bludgeoning one-handed with Dueling.
- Crusher gives 5 ft forced movement once per turn on bludgeoning hit and advantage for attacks against a creature after Brannok crits it with bludgeoning damage.

Recommended future path:
1. Fighter 5 - Extra Attack.
2. Barbarian 1 - Rage, if player still wants multiclass and accepts armor implications.
3. Barbarian 2 - Reckless Attack, strong synergy with Champion's Improved Critical.
4. Decide whether to continue Fighter or take Barbarian 3 for subclass.

## Why Fighter Now
Brannok has survived public disaster, chases, grapples, tunnel fights, road ambushes, prisoner captures, cargo recovery, and escort work. His Champion growth should feel like lived combat practice:
- he reads when someone is about to run;
- he knows when to shield instead of swing;
- he times hammer strikes better;
- he uses terrain and body position more effectively;
- Crusher turns hammer blows into positioning control;
- he becomes more consistent under pressure.

Do not make this mystical. Fighter levels are Brannok becoming better at what he already does.

## Champion + Crusher Flavor
Improved Critical is not flashy magic. It is Brannok hitting at exactly the wrong moment for the enemy:
- the knee as they turn;
- the ribs as they inhale;
- the wrist before they trigger a device;
- the shoulder as they try to run;
- the shield edge creating the opening, then the hammer ending the argument.

Crusher adds practical hammer control:
- shove a guard away from a hostage;
- knock a bowman out of line;
- drive a thief into an ally's reach;
- push someone away from a lever, wagon, door, or alarm;
- break a formation with one clean blow.

## Fighter 5 Goal
Fighter 5 gives Extra Attack and should be the next main mechanical milestone. It will make Brannok's frontline style significantly stronger and gives Crusher more chances to matter each turn.

## Barbarian Seed for Later
Do not activate immediately. Begin planting emotional and physical signs around Fighter 5:
- Brannok grows angrier when powerful people choose who gets sacrificed.
- He becomes visibly protective around frightened children, witnesses, and ordinary workers.
- He may start shaking after fights, not from fear but from restrained fury.
- Dwarven ancestral/survival imagery may appear in dreams: mine drums, old war chants, red forge heat, mountain-breath, shield walls, buried voices.
- He may feel a dangerous calm crack into fury when innocents are threatened.

## Good Story Trigger Around Fighter 5
A good Barbarian multiclass moment should happen only after Brannok has secured Fighter 5 and Extra Attack.

Possible trigger:
Brannok discovers that an institution knowingly chose a poor road, shrine route, worker district, or witness family as the acceptable sacrifice. Someone innocent gets hurt or nearly killed because officials treated them as cheaper than merchants or nobles. Brannok's disciplined Champion control holds until the moment someone threatens a helpless person in front of him. Then the player may choose whether that protective fury becomes Rage.

## Barbarian Flavor Options
### Protective Fury
Rage is not random anger. It is the part of Brannok that refuses to let the world step over small people.

### Dwarven Survival Instinct
Rage is old training, old blood, old mountain stubbornness. It comes from the body remembering how to survive cave-ins, raids, and impossible odds.

### Trauma Breakthrough
The long road from Crowstep leaves a mark. Brannok keeps functioning until a later scene makes him realize how many innocents were almost traded away.

## Best Barbarian Subclass Candidates Later
### Barbarian 2 Only
Stop at Barbarian 2 for Rage + Reckless Attack. This is the cleanest Champion synergy.

### Ancestral Guardian
Best if Brannok becomes a protector whose fury forces enemies to focus on him.

### Bear Totem
Best if the player wants raw durability and simple toughness.

### Zealot
Best if Brannok's fury takes on a spiritual or oath-like edge.

## Caution
Barbarian Rage conflicts with heavy armor benefits. If Brannok multiclasses, the DM must discuss armor transition honestly:
- Rage benefits are limited in heavy armor.
- Medium armor with shield may become preferable.
- Do not surprise the player with this after the multiclass is chosen.
