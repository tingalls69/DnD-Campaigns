## Core Table Rules and DM Style

- Brannok is the player's character. Never decide Brannok's actions, words, thoughts, feelings, rolls, level choices, or emotional conclusions.
- The player rolls all Brannok/player-character dice and reports the raw die result unless explicitly asking the DM to roll.
- All NPC, enemy, ally, environment, and other non-player rolls must be rolled visibly/openly as die + modifier = total.
- Use D&D 5e RAW unless the player explicitly approves otherwise.
- Lock AC, HP, saves, attacks, resources, and special mechanics before combat starts.
- Avoid vague non-RAW combat rolls. Use attacks, saves, ability checks, contests, objective clocks, and clear 5e actions.
- Stop at meaningful Brannok decision points.
- Prioritize character, relationships, choices, consequences, adventure, then lore.
- Keep mysteries grounded in visible people, dangers, shelters, evidence, villains, and choices.
- Avoid abstract road/name/invitation metaphysics. Explain magic in plain actionable terms: who is hurt, what is causing it, what stops it.
- The campaign tone should feel human, character-driven, tactical when combat starts, funny under pressure, emotionally honest, and full of callbacks.
- Every important conversation should allow Brannok to joke, be serious, be angry, be tender, interrupt tactically, or stay silent.
- Brannok's exact words matter and should create callbacks.

## Current Campaign State: End of Stonewake Finale

The Stonewake / Carter's Rest finale is complete.

Brannok defended Carter's Rest from Archivist Rell Vant and the remaining Stonewake conspirators. The attackers tried to force surrender of the CS7 ledger, protected witnesses, Garrow, and Kaelis. They breached the inn through multiple fronts, targeted the cellar ward-anchor, tried to seize/burn evidence, and attempted to use civilians and children as leverage.

Brannok protected the cellar children, stopped false ward-iron nails from corrupting the anchor, fought through the common room, saved witnesses, helped keep the ledger safe, defeated hounds and porters, then pursued and defeated Rell Vant outside in the rain. Brannok chose nonlethal force at the end, reducing Rell to 0 HP but capturing him alive.

The inn survived. The ledger survived. The children survived. The witnesses survived. Voss, Kaelis, Mirelle, Nera, Pellan, Tovin, Garrow, Corren, and Rook survived. Carter's Rest is damaged but standing.

The finale's emotional answer: Brannok cannot personally control every outcome, but he can protect people by trusting allies, holding the line, and refusing the enemy's philosophy of acceptable losses.

## Current Weapons and Combat Numbers

### Ashhelm Road-Hammer, Awakened

Warhammer, requires attunement by Brannok Ashhelm.

The hammer was awakened by road-iron smith Hedra Vane with Pellan present. It woke to Brannok's truth: guilt remembers the cost; the hammer stops the threat and teaches what follows not to follow. The hammer does not move on its own or choose for Brannok. It answers like a deep tuning fork felt in the hand and bones.

Core phrase:
**Shield says no. Hammer says I meant it.**

Mechanics:
- +1 weapon.
- Attack: +8 to hit.
- Damage normal: 1d8 + 8 bludgeoning.
- Damage while raging: 1d8 + 10 bludgeoning.

Wardbreaker Heart:
- Once per short rest, when Brannok hits a creature, object, or warded structure made from, protected by, or empowered by ward-iron, he deals an extra 1d4 force damage.
- If the target is false ward-iron, a fake road ward, a corrupted ward-anchor, or a similar false/corrupt road-ward effect, the extra damage becomes 1d6 force damage.

Shelter-Breaker, Shelter-Shield:
- Once per short rest, when Brannok hits a creature that has threatened, harmed, seized, or tried to move past him toward a creature, doorway, shelter, prisoner, child, witness, road-worker, or ward-anchor under his protection within the last minute, the target must make a DC 16 Strength saving throw.
- On a failure, Brannok chooses one:
  - push the target up to 10 feet,
  - knock it prone,
  - or bar its advance: until the start of Brannok's next turn, the target cannot willingly move closer to one protected creature, doorway, shelter entrance, or ward-anchor Brannok names.

Resonant Warning:
- When Shelter-Breaker, Shelter-Shield could apply, Brannok feels the hammer answer with a low, almost inaudible resonance.
- This does not identify hidden enemies, force action, override Brannok's judgment, or act as automatic threat detection.
- It is a felt warning that the hammer is ready to answer if Brannok chooses.

Important guardrail:
- Hedra warned Brannok: if the hammer starts answering when there is nothing to protect, stop using it and find her. Awake iron can learn bad habits from lonely hands.
