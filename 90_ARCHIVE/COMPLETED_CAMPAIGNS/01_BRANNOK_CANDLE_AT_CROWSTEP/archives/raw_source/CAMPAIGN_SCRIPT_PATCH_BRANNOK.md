# Campaign Script Patch - Brannok / Simplified Road Ward Mystery

This patch updates the original script for the Brannok run of The Candle at Crowstep.

## Core Correction
The story should not focus on abstract debt magic. The road mystery is a practical sabotage and public-safety problem.

## Updated Act I Outcome
The failed Crowstep public ritual was a weekly road-ward safety check. It failed because the seventh road ward component was missing, damaged, corrupted, or bypassed. Records were altered so the person responsible for catching the problem, Mara Vell, was removed from the chain.

Brannok helped uncover:
- Mara's name was erased.
- Voss delayed a warning.
- Third Bell-Marrow exploited the cover-up.
- Replacement ward chalk was moved through hidden tunnels.
- Marrow attempted to sacrifice Saint Orra's west approach to preserve the other weak routes.

## Current End of Act I / Transition Beat
Brannok sleeps at the Crowstep watch-house after helping stop Marrow. While he rests, Mara, Calder, Nessa, Perrin, and watch support secure the ward-control room.

When play resumes, do not immediately launch another chase. Start with aftermath:
1. Brannok wakes from long rest.
2. Update resources and level 3 Champion features.
3. Give a clear report from Sister Vale, Mara, or Calder.
4. Present practical consequences:
  - Saint Orra road may be restricted.
  - Crowstep public trust is unstable.
  - Voss and Marrow are prisoners.
  - Third Bell cells may still be active.
  - The roads need real repair, not secrecy.
5. Offer downtime/relationship choices before the next danger unless the player rushes.

## Next Story Direction
The next act should widen from Crowstep to regional consequences while staying understandable.

### Practical Threats
- Road closures delay food, ore, pilgrims, medicine, and pay.
- Some towns want the truth hidden to avoid panic.
- Some factions want the roads open despite danger.
- Third Bell cells may try to recover supplies, kill witnesses, or destroy evidence.
- The real deeper source remains hidden, but do not reveal it through abstract lore dumps.

### Good Next Scenes
- Watch-house aftermath and rest.
- Mara explaining the road-ward repair problem in plain terms.
- Brannok training/repairing gear, leaning into Fighter discipline.
- Public fallout: frightened townsfolk, Candle Court anger, road workers demanding answers.
- Interrogation of Voss or Marrow with concrete questions.
- A choice of which road/town to help first.
- A combat or rescue involving a real threatened caravan, shrine group, road crew, or witness.

## Keep the Original Spine, But Translate It
Original campaign themes can remain:
- Candle Court false blame.
- Third Bell committing smaller wrongs to delay a bigger disaster.
- Hidden bargain/source later.
- Moral choice about who pays.

But translate them into Brannok-playable terms:
- who is unsafe
- who lied
- who has supplies
- who needs guarding
- what road is closed
- who is trying to run
- who needs hammering

## Forbidden Going Forward
Do not introduce:
- legal ritual ownership of Brannok;
- written refusal procedures;
- debt bearer transfer mechanics;
- multi-step ritual grammar scenes;
- repeated vague warnings without practical explanation.

## Act II Direction for Brannok
Act II should start after recovery and fallout. It should likely involve Saint Orra's Crossing or Bellweather, depending on player choices.

Potential Act II premise:
The road wards are temporarily stabilized but not repaired. Crowstep can only send enough verified replacement stock and guards to fully secure one route immediately. Saint Orra's west approach has vulnerable pilgrims/smallholders. Bellweather East Cut has trade/caravan pressure. The South Road has recent damage and evidence. Brannok must decide where to go or who to trust with the other route.

## Barbarian Seed Timing
Do not push Barbarian yet. Use Act II to develop Brannok's Fighter discipline and protective identity. Around Fighter 5, a situation involving institutional sacrifice of innocents can offer the emotional opening for Rage.

## v11 Travel-to-Stonewake Patch
Brannok has moved beyond the original Act I aftermath. Crowstep is now mostly aftermath and launch point. The current active scene is the North Road wayhouse rest during travel to Stonewake Market.

Do not resume from the old Crowstep watch-house long rest unless explicitly asked to rewind. Resume from the wayhouse wagon rest and continue toward Stonewake if the player proceeds.

The old tollhouse marked-cargo incident is now canon:
- three cuts on a mile marker means marked cargo;
- cleanup thieves were captured;
- stolen wagon/cargo was recovered;
- a green-lantern buyer escaped toward Stonewake contacts.

Use this as a Stonewake-facing lead, not a new local mini-arc.
