# Future Conflict Plan - Brannok / Candle at Crowstep

This is a DM-facing plan to keep the story from being improvised into needless complexity.

## Design Goal
Keep future conflicts simple and classic fantasy:
- bad roads allow goblins, raiders, beasts, monsters, and thieves to attack;
- corrupt people profit from unsafe roads;
- Brannok protects ordinary people and grows as a Fighter;
- villains have understandable motives.

## Current Act II Theme
**Who profits when the roads are unsafe**

The road wards are damaged but not destroyed. Crowstep can repair them, but repair teams, merchants, and witnesses are vulnerable. Criminal factions want to exploit the gap.

## Current Story State
Brannok has left Crowstep and is one night into the road to Stonewake Market. He recovered stolen cargo at the old tollhouse and preserved a green-lantern buyer lead. The buyer fled north/side-track and should feed into Stonewake rather than become a separate endless chase.

## Crowstep Crest Cell
The local Crowstep Crest operation is mostly defeated. Captured/implicated locals include major Crest actors and hirelings. Use Crowstep Crest as consequence/evidence, not as an endless local enemy generator.

## Next Antagonist Layer: Stonewake Buyer Network
A practical criminal-market network buying stolen ward iron, selling fake safe-route goods, fencing stolen cargo, and exploiting road panic.

### Motive
Profit and control. If the network controls safe-route information, fake ward tokens, and stolen ward iron, they can decide who feels safe, who pays, and who bleeds.

### Methods
- buy stolen ward iron;
- sell fake road-safety charms/tokens;
- mark cargo with road signs like three cuts;
- hire cleanup crews and tollhouse thieves;
- use green lanterns or code phrases like "clean wool" for handoffs;
- bribe or intimidate minor clerks, guards, and brokers.

### Personality of Network
Practical, mercantile, and predatory. Not occult masterminds. They are dangerous because ordinary people trust safety marks and route papers.

## Active Green-Lantern Lead
- Signal: green lantern on a hooded cart.
- Code phrase: "clean wool."
- Context: buyer expected stolen cargo at old tollhouse; fled when Brannok recovered it and wayhouse witnesses/guards appeared.
- Direction: north/side track toward Stonewake contacts.

Use in Stonewake:
- a cart with green-lantern wax residue;
- a broker who asks if goods are "clean wool";
- a market guard who recognizes the signal as used by cargo fences;
- Mirelle Thorn identifying the phrase or connected buyer.

## Field Enemy Types
- hired blades;
- corrupt caravan guards;
- fake charm sellers;
- smugglers;
- raiders and goblins hired or misled by humans;
- beasts exploiting weak roads;
- cleanup crews trying to destroy witnesses/proof.

Most enemies should have a role, motive, tactic, and maybe one clue. Then move on.

## Planned Next Hook: Stonewake Market
The next major hook should happen after Brannok completes the wayhouse rest and reaches Stonewake.

Possible Stonewake opening scenes:
1. Fake road-safety charm dispute in the market.
2. Hired blades trying to seize Mirelle Thorn's ledger case.
3. A green-lantern cart spotted near a south-gate cargo yard.
4. A caravan widow being cheated by a broker selling fake route seals.
5. Sergeant Elian Voss reluctantly recognizing Brannok's Crowstep writ.

## Companion Hook: Mirelle Thorn
Introduce Mirelle through action, not exposition.

Best options:
- She is arguing with a charm-seller over fake safety tokens when hired blades try to take her ledger.
- She recognizes the phrase "clean wool" and realizes Brannok has proof she needs.
- She has social access and backline support skills; Brannok has proof, credibility, and hammer authority.

Do not force her into the party. Make cooperation useful and character-driven.

## Character Growth Beats
Use downtime and road missions to grow Brannok:
- practicing Crusher-style hammer control;
- protecting wagon drivers, witnesses, and backline allies;
- seeing fake safety hurt ordinary people;
- remembering the mine collapse when records or safety marks are falsified;
- being told by others that his practical judgment is not stupidity.

## Fighter 5 Path
Before Barbarian, emphasize:
- improved timing;
- better control rather than just brute force;
- protecting allies;
- reading battlefields;
- decisive strikes;
- Crusher forced movement as learned hammer craft.

## Barbarian Seed After Fighter 5
Only after Fighter 5, offer a story trigger if it fits:
- Brannok sees a poor road or civilian group chosen as acceptable loss;
- raiders threaten children, pilgrims, workers, or a vulnerable ally;
- a corrupt authority treats lives as numbers;
- Brannok's disciplined Champion control cracks into protective fury.

The player chooses whether to multiclass.

## Enemy Simplicity Reminder
Most bad guys should have:
- a role;
- a motive;
- a tactic;
- maybe one clue.

Then move on. Do not name or deepen every prisoner.
