# The Last Lantern - Revised Story Arc

## Core Thesis

Powerful people are using roads and records to decide who gets abandoned.

The Last Lantern becomes the company that refuses to leave people behind.

## Main Villain Structure

### O. Thresh / Orvaine Thresh

O. Thresh is the institutional villain. He is terrifying because he can make disappearance look official.

He uses old Road Works channels, sealed routing indexes, transfer orders, closure approvals, false safety designations, custody pressure, and legal uncertainty.

His horror is simple:

He can close a road on paper and make people vanish in the real world.

### South Lantern

South Lantern is the immediate visible villain. She is the face, voice, and knife of the first arc.

She believes roads do not need light; they need obedience. She believes frightened people are easier to move. She believes civilian courage is dangerous.

She should appear or speak soon.

## Act I - Greybridge: The Town That Must Choose

Status: mostly completed, with interrogation and planning fallout still active.

The party defeated the physical attack and then stopped the enemy's official-pressure follow-up. The danger became custody theft by paperwork, backed by knives when exposed.

Completed core objective:

- Keep the Hearthline witnesses in Greybridge.
- Prove the transfer order was false/altered.
- Secure the records.
- Capture Halven Rusk and the masked coordinator alive.
- Prevent Pella Morn from being taken.
- Gain a clear lead to Old Bellweather House / South Lantern Relay.

Current Greybridge fallout:

- interrogate Rusk;
- interrogate or examine the masked coordinator;
- identify the South Lantern Continuity Office;
- understand the command mask and road-arch token network;
- decide how much to ask of Pella;
- plan the Bellweather approach;
- secure Greybridge's witnesses and prisoners before leaving.

Act I should not linger into fictional legal debate. Paperwork was a weapon; now use the evidence to drive a rescue-forward Bellweather mission.

## Act II - Road To Bellweather House

Transition from Greybridge to the adventure site while showing the enemy is watching.

Include one concrete road hazard or tactical obstacle:

- broken bridge;
- false ward marker;
- trapped lantern post;
- road collapse;
- cleanup-team ambush;
- frightened local guide;
- Pella's hidden service route beneath rain steps and ash trough.

Include one emotional travel beat:

- Brannok sees something that reminds him of Carter's Rest/Nera.
- Selene's closed-eye charm reacts near a threshold or old relay marker.
- Rook makes a frightened child or young courier laugh.
- Corren jokes badly under pressure, then does something brave.

Show South Lantern's attention:

- crossed-flame mark;
- cane tap through a relay bell;
- warning for "the dwarf in the gray cap";
- warning for "the woman with the closed eye."

## Act III - Bellweather House: The Relay That Still Rings

Bellweather House is the first major dungeon/adventure site.

It must contain rescuable people, not just evidence.

Objective:

Rescue the trapped people, recover South Lantern's field ledger, and prevent the relay bell from sending a false route order before South Lantern burns the evidence.

Mandatory elements:

- burned wing from old structural fire;
- sealed Road Works archive room;
- ward relay chamber;
- bell tower or signal chamber;
- hidden servant-road entrance beneath the rain steps and behind the old ash trough;
- false safety markers;
- unlit lantern symbol;
- field ledger or torn ledger pages;
- tactical fight near the bell/relay;
- hazard where breaking the wrong thing destroys evidence or harms captives;
- a situation where the party must split responsibilities.

Pella should not be treated as a necessary key or dragged into danger by default. Her knowledge can guide planning, mapping, or entry, but whether she goes near Bellweather must be a player-facing choice with clear risk. The south bell second-hearing threat should make her involvement dangerous, not automatic.

## Companion Spotlight Requirements

### Brannok

Give him a moment where he cannot do everything himself. He must choose what to hold personally and what to trust others to handle.

### Selene

Give her a threshold/patron moment tied to her Archfey rebuild. Her growth is becoming consequence without becoming control.

### Marcella

Give her a social/control encounter: stopping panic, turning South Lantern's rhetoric against her, humiliating a field commander, or using Cutting Words/Bardic Inspiration at a pivotal moment.

### Mara / Bellbreaker

Give her a bell, threshold, or door. Bellbreaker belongs to Mara. She may need to physically hold a door, break a relay bell, or defend trapped people.

### Needle / Nessa

Give her a stealth route, hidden hostage, or ambush-prevention task.

### Zula

Give her ward architecture, tattoo/ward counterwork, and magical relay systems. Let her identify what part is dangerous.

### Mirelle

Give her records, forged orders, sealed route indexes, and contradictions. Let her discover the legal lie.

### Corren

Give him a grappler/practical protector moment: restrain a runner, hold a door, drag someone from collapse, or pin a dangerous enemy while joking badly.

### Rook

Give him a small morale moment with frightened children, a young courier, or trapped victims.

### Ves

Let Ves scout, sting, warn Selene, and react strongly to threshold magic.

## Act IV - South Lantern Contact

South Lantern should not wait until the final boss to appear.

Use one of these:

1. Relay Bell Scene - South Lantern speaks through the bell.
2. Parley Scene - South Lantern offers to trade hostages for records.
3. Abandonment Scene - South Lantern abandons or punishes her own team.
4. Cane Scene - the black cane with silver unlit-lantern cap appears before she fully enters.

She should recognize:

- Brannok makes civilians brave.
- Selene understands thresholds and choices.
- The Last Lantern is dangerous because it gives people somewhere to stand.

## Act V - Public Consequence / Exposing The Road

After Bellweather House, the party should have enough evidence to force a public or institutional choice.

Possible next beats:

- Greybridge formally defies a transfer order.
- Carter's Rest/Stonewake gathers survivors at the sanctuary.
- Saint Orveth's Reliquary is threatened by official supply disruption or messenger manipulation.
- Thresh reacts by isolating one safe location.
- South Lantern becomes personally aggressive.
- The Last Lantern name becomes known among survivors and road people.

## Story Style Guardrails

Do not make this primarily a conspiracy puzzle.

Make it a rescue-forward fantasy adventure with people in danger, visible villains, tactical choices, companion spotlights, jokes under pressure, and real consequences.
