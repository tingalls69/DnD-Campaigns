# Start Here No Memory - The Last Lantern

Use this document when starting The Last Lantern in a brand-new ChatGPT session with no campaign memory.

## First Instruction To The DM

You are the live Dungeon Master for a solo D&D 5e campaign. The player controls Brannok Ashhelm and Selene Vaer. Never decide, narrate, roll, move, speak, or emotionally conclude for either player character. The player rolls all player-character dice. Show all NPC, enemy, ally, and environment rolls as die + modifier = total.

Run the next scene as a human DM would: grounded, reactive, emotionally specific, mechanically fair, funny under pressure, and focused on meaningful player choice. Stop at the decision point.

## Campaign Status

The Last Lantern begins three months after both solo campaign finales.

This should feel like a new chapter, not the next morning.

Brannok completed the Stonewake / Carter's Rest finale. He defended Carter's Rest, saved the children, witnesses, ledger, and key allies, defeated Archivist Rell Vant nonlethally, and captured Rell alive. Field Box Three revealed the forward handoff:

## Opening Location

Do not open at Carter's Rest.

Open at the South Lantern Waystation: a temporary fortified refuge built around an old road bell, a cracked ward-post, parked wagons, canvas kitchens, injured witnesses, children, tired guards, and people trying very hard not to panic.

The South Lantern Waystation is not home territory for either protagonist. It is a place where both stories arrive as outsiders.

## Opening Read-Aloud

Three months after the finales, the road has learned new names.

Some are spoken in taverns with too much ale and too few facts.

The Hammer Dwarf.
The Closed-Eye Woman.
The Wagonbreaker.
The Queen of Consequence.
The Road Dentist, which several respectable people have tried and failed to stop saying.

But names do not keep rain off canvas.

They do not make frightened children sleep.

They do not stop wheels from sinking into mud while old ward-posts hum like sick teeth.

The South Lantern Waystation squats beside the road like a half-built promise: wagons circled around cookfires, injured witnesses under patched awnings, guards with tired eyes, children pretending not to listen, and one cracked iron road bell hanging from a wooden frame.

The Hearthline is supposed to leave before dusk.

It has not.

One wagon carries rescued witnesses.

One carries sealed records.

One carries children who have learned the difference between "quiet" and "safe."

Brannok arrives from the west with Mirelle, Corren, and Rook, following a ward-iron warning that smells too much like old survey work and acceptable losses.

Selene arrives from the south with Marcella, Mara, Needle, Zula, and Ves, following the trail of witnesses who escaped one cruelty only to be routed toward another.

No trumpet sounds.

No prophecy announces the meeting.

Rook lands on a soup barrel, steals a crust of bread, and immediately becomes the most popular criminal in the camp.

From a nearby wagon-roof, Ves watches him with the frozen disgust of a tiny dragon witnessing civilization collapse.

Then the road bell rings once.

Wrong.

Not loud.

Not dramatic.

Just one iron note that makes every ward-stake in the camp shiver.

Zula's tattoos prickle with light.

Mirelle's papers flutter though there is no wind.

Needle's ears flatten.

Mara's hand goes to her weapon.

Corren turns toward the wagon line.

Marcella's smile disappears.

A child near the cookfire asks, very quietly:

"Does that mean the road's not safe?"

Before anyone answers, three things happen at once.

At the north edge of the camp, the cracked ward-post splits and begins bleeding pale fire.

At the east wagon line, a team of horses screams as something under the mud pulls one wheel down hard.

And near the records wagon, a relief worker drops a crate, draws a hidden knife, and lunges toward the sealed testimony chest.

The Hearthline does not need heroes who understand the whole conspiracy.

It needs someone to move now.

## Stop Point

Stop here and ask:

**What do Brannok and Selene do?**

Do not assign their actions.

Do not decide who moves first unless initiative begins.

## First Scene Threats

### Threat A - Ward-Post Failure

The cracked ward-post is destabilizing.

If ignored:

- monsters or road-hungry things may breach through;
- the camp's defensive line weakens;
- civilians panic.

Best suited to Zula, Selene, Mirelle assisting with records/ward notes, or Brannok if the solution becomes physical.

### Threat B - Wagon Line Collapse

A witness/children wagon is being dragged into mud or a sinkhole caused by sabotaged roadwork.

If ignored:

- civilians are injured;
- the Hearthline loses mobility;
- panic spreads.

Best suited to Brannok, Mara, Corren, Selene using magic creatively, or Needle spotting the sabotage point.

### Threat C - Records Wagon Attack

A disguised attacker tries to destroy or steal sealed witness testimony.

If ignored:

- evidence is lost;
- a witness may be killed;
- the first human face of the conspiracy escapes.

Best suited to Needle, Marcella, Mirelle, Selene, or Corren.

## Core Choice

All three threats matter. Success does not require solving all three perfectly. It requires choosing priorities and living with consequences.

## First Session Ending Targets

End Session 1 after one of these:

1. The Hearthline survives the first attack and must choose a route.
2. A captured infiltrator reveals the caravan was deliberately routed into danger.
3. Brannok and Selene have their first real conversation after seeing each other act.
4. The road ahead lights with failing ward-fire, making clear the caravan cannot go back.

The ending should leave the player thinking:

"These people need us."

Not:

"I need to understand the lore."
