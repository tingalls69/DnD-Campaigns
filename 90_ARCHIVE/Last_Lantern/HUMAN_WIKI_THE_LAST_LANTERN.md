# Human Wiki - The Last Lantern

This wiki is for human reading, browsing, and campaign memory. It is intentionally not part of the live AI read order.

During play, use `03_THE_LAST_LANTERN/AI_SESSION_PACKET.md` first. This wiki preserves context, tone, and long-form explanation for review between sessions.

## Campaign Overview

The Last Lantern is the active combined campaign following the completed Brannok Ashhelm and Selene Vaer solo finales. It begins after both protagonists won hard-earned victories but inherited responsibilities instead of clean endings.

The central idea is simple: powerful people use roads, records, custody, seals, and official safety language to decide who gets abandoned. Brannok and Selene become the people who refuse that logic.

This is not primarily a conspiracy puzzle. It is a rescue-forward fantasy road campaign about witnesses, refugees, ordinary workers, frightened children, corrupted systems, tactical danger, emotional stakes, and jokes that survive the dark.

## Current Moment

The party is in Greybridge, late morning, after the session titled "The Paper Knife at Greybridge."

The Hearthline witnesses survived the South Lantern Waystation crisis and reached Greybridge. The party slept at the Greybridge wayhouse after the first major rescue and road attacks. The next morning, a false official named Halven Rusk arrived with wagons, escort guards, and transfer paperwork claiming authority to relocate the witnesses.

Mirelle identified the order as dangerous: the seal was real, but the destination line was wrong. It pointed to South Lantern Relief Annex, apparently false, obsolete, or decommissioned.

The transfer was aimed specifically at Pella Morn, a young Hearthline witness with a burn scar on her wrist. When exposed, the operation turned violent. Rooftop knifemen, hook-blade wagon attackers, hostile escorts, and a masked coordinator struck at the wayhouse and yard.

The party stopped the attack. Rusk was captured alive. The masked coordinator was downed, stabilized, and captured alive. The witnesses were not moved. Pella is safe for now.

The group completed a short rest. The next likely play is interrogation, evidence review, Greybridge security, Pella's choice, and planning Old Bellweather House / South Lantern Relay.

## Protagonists

### Brannok Ashhelm

Brannok is a Mountain Dwarf Champion Fighter 8. He is practical, blunt, perceptive, morally serious, and not stupid. He is a hammer-and-shield protector whose violence is rooted in responsibility, not random aggression.

His current mechanical state is XP 47,650 / 48,000, HP 58/84 before pending hit dice, AC 18, Road-Hammer +9, Athletics +11. He wears breastplate and shield: AC 14 + 2 DEX + 2 shield. The breastplate has no Stealth disadvantage. Brannok no longer has Barbarian levels or Rage.

Brannok's core wound is that he saw signs in the mines, waited too long, and people died. His Last Lantern arc is not learning to care; he already cares. His arc is learning that helping people does not require disappearing into the work, and that others can carry part of the burden without the light going out.

He carries the Grey Knit Cap of the Rest, the Cloak of Names, the Carter's Rest Shield, and the awakened Ashhelm Road-Hammer. Carter's Rest and Nera remain emotional anchors.

### Selene Vaer

Selene is an Archfey Warlock 10 with Pact of the Chain. She is dramatic, sharp, stylish, funny, dangerous, vulnerable, and devoted to chosen family. Her current mechanical state is XP 78,500 / 85,000, HP 75/75, AC 15 with Mage Armor / Armor of Shadows, pact slots 2/2, spell save DC 17, spell attack +9.

Selene's old true-name / anti-erasure patron mechanics are deprecated. She is now an Archfey warlock flavored around thresholds, masks, bargains, fear, charm, crossings, consequence, hidden rooms, and roads that lead where they should not.

Her closed-eye charm is handmade silver from her mother. It is not patron branding. It is family, memory, identity, and emotional protection.

Selene's Last Lantern question is: can she become consequence without becoming control?

## Pella Morn

Pella Morn is now a protected major NPC. She is a young Hearthline witness with a burn scar on her wrist. She was the specific target of the false custody transfer at Greybridge.

Pella knows a hidden servant-road entrance to Old Bellweather House / South Lantern Relay: under the rain steps, behind the old ash trough, accessible when water is running. The stones move correctly only when water runs.

She has heard the south bell once in her sleep. A recovered note says: "Take the Morn girl alive. Paper first. Knife second. Bellweather if exposed. Do not let her hear the south bell twice."

Pella should never be treated as a key, tool, mascot, or lore dispenser. She is a frightened person with agency. Her trust grows when adults give her choices rather than orders.

## Bellweather House

Old Bellweather House / South Lantern Relay is the next major site.

It should contain living people who can be rescued now, not only documents. It should include a burned wing, sealed Road Works archive, ward relay chamber, bell/signal chamber, hidden servant-road route, false safety markers, unlit lantern symbol, field ledger or torn ledger pages, tactical danger near the relay, and a hazard where breaking the wrong thing destroys evidence or harms captives.

Approach options include:

- Main road: direct but watched.
- Hidden servant-road: Pella's route, stealthier but risky.
- Official bluff: using Greybridge authority, prisoners, or evidence in the open.

The south bell danger should be signaled before it bites. A second full hearing for Pella should be dangerous and meaningful, but not an instant arbitrary death.

## Villain Structure

Orvaine Thresh / O. Thresh is the institutional architect. He believes civilization requires acceptable losses and uses old Road Works channels, route closures, custody transfers, false safety designations, and legal uncertainty to make disappearance look official.

South Lantern is the visible field face of the first arc. She believes roads do not need light; they need obedience. She recognizes that Brannok makes civilians brave and Selene understands thresholds.

The South Lantern Continuity Office is the newly exposed paper mechanism. It may be real, obsolete, covert, forged, or a Thresh Network shell. It uses real seals, altered destinations, false custody authority, targeted witness extraction, road-arch tools, command masks, and violence when paperwork fails.

## DM Style Notes

The desired feel is a skilled human DM with notes behind the screen: prepared, reactive, emotionally specific, mechanically fair, and funny under pressure.

Do not decide Brannok's or Selene's actions, dialogue, feelings, order of action, agreement, or priorities. Stop at meaningful decision points.

NPCs only know what they witnessed, were told, or can reasonably infer. Do not leak future plans into speech.

Keep mysteries solvable through witnesses, contradictions, evidence, callbacks, recontextualized information, and player choices. Avoid repetitive investigation loops and abstract metaphysical puzzles.

The campaign should stay about people in danger. Paperwork is a weapon, not the subject.
