# Current State Bridge - What The Crossover Must Inherit

## Brannok side - current truth

Brannok Ashhelm is in the final Stonewake local act. This is a superseded pre-finale combat snapshot: **Mountain Dwarf Fighter 5 Champion / Barbarian 2, level 7, AC 18, HP 75/75, XP 31,950 / 34,000**, with Rage 2/2, Action Surge, Second Wind, Reckless Attack, Danger Sense, the Ashhelm Road-Hammer, shield, handaxes, and the Ward-Iron Brace. Current Last Lantern Brannok is Level 8, HP 92/92, AC 18 from breastplate / Pellan's gifted medium armor + DEX + shield (14 + 2 + 2), no Stealth disadvantage, rage-compatible.

He has already moved from ordinary corruption into visible high-fantasy road-ward danger. The old bridge ward-gate, Red Willow Crossing, South Toll Cut, East Ridge Survey Store, and Control Node Seven showed that the Stonewake conspiracy was only one node in a wider road-control system.

### Brannok's emotional state entering the crossover

He has proven, repeatedly, that he does not wait anymore. He acts. He breaks doors. He protects children, witnesses, drivers, clerks, and people who were almost filed into disappearance. He is still carrying mine-collapse guilt, but his rage has become more disciplined: not a tantrum, not bloodlust, but the shield deciding when the hammer moves.

The crossover should not reset him to "rough dwarf with hammer." Brannok is:

- blunt, funny, protective, and morally serious;
- suspicious of polished authority and fake safety;
- capable of mercy when a person is a weasel rather than a rat;
- haunted by what happens when decent people wait too long;
- increasingly aware that paperwork can be a weapon;
- uncomfortable with magic, but willing to learn what keeps people alive.

### Brannok's unresolved crossover thread

The active thread is:

**▲ | CS7 | RW-ROUTING | IRON TRANSFER AUTHORIZED - O. THRESH, COMMISSIONER (RET.)**

Control Node Seven proved someone was still operating old road-authority hardware. The iron was moving through Stonewake, not just to Stonewake. A retired official named Thresh is the clean forward hook. Do not explain him immediately. Brannok should enter the crossover with a practical question: *who is using old road authority to decide which roads live and which people vanish*

## Selene side - current truth

Selene Vaer and the Daughters are rushing toward Vellford. The newest handoff state has Selene as **Warlock 10, HP 75/75, pact slots 2/2**, with the feature **Witness That Cannot Be Erased**, the Champagne Consequence look, and a current arc focused on names, witnesses, refusal of erasure, and direct action.

The Daughters' current roles:

- Kara: shield, protection, frontline, temple corruption anger.
- Eya: healing, calm, survivor care, truth with gentleness.
- Jasna: records, evidence, legal pressure, refusal to let papers erase people.
- Zula: wards, counter-magic, analysis, practical magical threat control.
- Needle: scout, infiltration, traps, locks, roofs, dangerous laughter.
- Mara: protection, grapples, practical force, anti-escape wall.
- Marcella: social pressure, room control, style, dignity, romance with Selene.
- Ves: cat-sized pseudodragon, cat personality, fiercely attached to Selene, not a cat.

### Selene's emotional state entering the crossover

Selene has shifted away from hiding behind borrowed faces and toward being remembered. She is theatrical, sharp, wounded, funny, terrifying when cornered, and deeply loyal to the Daughters. Her closed-eye charm and identity/witness themes matter, but the current campaign preference is clear: these themes should show up through victims, choices, and consequences, not abstract phrase-law.

The crossover should not reduce Selene to "charismatic warlock with trauma." She is:

- a leader who still fears what names can do;
- a survivor who turns style into armor;
- a woman learning that being witnessed is not the same as being owned;
- romantic with Marcella without becoming only a romance plot;
- powerful enough to scare rooms and vulnerable enough to need the Daughters;
- someone who makes cruelty answer for itself in public.

### Selene's unresolved crossover threads

Before the solo finale ends, two unresolved threads should remain visible but unexplained:

1. A Choir field captain slips away carrying a sealed route document stamped with an official Road Works Survey seal, not Choir magic.
2. A rescued prisoner transported from far away carries a final routing document with a road-warden commercial cipher the Daughters do not recognize.

These do not reveal Thresh. They only prove the Choir is being moved by secular logistics, not just cult devotion.

## Shared start condition for The Iron Road

When the combined campaign begins, both protagonists should have finished their solo finales and be carrying different halves of the same truth:

- Brannok knows the roads are being controlled through old ward infrastructure and institutional logistics.
- Selene knows people are being disappeared through transfer routes, false care, and secular paperwork hiding behind cult language.

Neither knows the other's full story. Neither should immediately trust the other. Both should recognize competence.
