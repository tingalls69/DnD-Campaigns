# The Iron Road - Current Story Bible

## Logline

Two heroes who have been fighting the same forty-year crime from opposite ends meet over a dead road courier at a sabotaged ward-gate. Brannok has followed the money, iron, and broken roads. Selene has followed the missing people, false names, and erased witnesses. The truth is that the roads and the disappearances are one machine.

## The thesis

A road is a promise: leave here, arrive there, survive the between.

Thresh learned that if you control the promise, you control who counts. You do not need to murder every enemy. You can close their road, redirect their cart, misfile their transfer, mark their bridge unsafe, sell their family a false detour, and let hunger, fear, monsters, and distance do the work.

Brannok's answer: stop waiting and break the machine.

Selene's answer: make the erased count and put the machine on record.

The campaign needs both.

## The villain

### Orvaine Thresh, Commissioner Retired

Thresh is not a cult prophet. He is worse: an old institutional operator who discovered that logistics is a kind of violence nobody notices until the bodies are already cold.

He began as a repair-minded official during a period of failing roads and corrupt budgets. He did save people once. That matters. The evil comes from what he did after: he decided his judgment mattered more than public truth, more than consent, more than lives he could label as acceptable loss.

His current project uses three layers:

1. **Legal paperwork:** transfer orders, road closure notices, freight exceptions, survey authority, custody overrides.
2. **Physical infrastructure:** ward-stones, road-gates, old survey stations, route markers, real ward-iron.
3. **Human predation:** Choir cells, false care networks, brokers, ledger-cleaners, guards, couriers, frightened clerks.

He knows parts of the occult side, but he does not understand the deepest old-road layer as well as he thinks. That hubris matters later.

### What Thresh wants

Not world destruction. Not chaos.

He wants a continent where movement is managed by people like him: where roads stay safe for useful commerce, dangerous for inconvenient witnesses, closed for poor towns, open for powerful patrons, and quietly redirected when someone needs to vanish.

His line, if he ever gets one:

> "You think roads are freedom because you walk them. Roads are power because someone decides where they go."

## The first meeting: Mirren's Crossing

The first combined session begins at Mirren's Crossing, a ward-bridge on the border between the two campaign regions.

### What is happening

A road courier is dead. The bridge ward-stones are damaged. A cargo cart is half-hanging over the side. One survivor is trapped beneath the axle. A sealed message tube is wedged under a cracked marker stone. The tube bears a route cipher connected to both current leads.

Brannok arrives because Road/Ward authorities or allies have sent him to inspect a suspected CS7-linked ward failure.

Selene arrives because a rescued witness or Vellford evidence named this crossing as a transfer point.

Both have valid reasons. Both can be right. Neither should be treated as intruding on the other's story.

### The immediate objective

Rescue the survivor, stabilize the bridge, secure the courier tube, and stop whoever returns to clean the scene.

No exposition dump. Start in motion.

## Act structure

### Act One - The Meeting of Roads

Fantasy dial: low.

The heroes investigate practical overlapping crimes: road sabotage, false transfers, missing people, and old survey seals. They distrust each other first, then realize they are chasing the same machine.

Core locations:

- Mirren's Crossing
- a frightened inn built at an old toll stop
- a Road Works archive room
- a service tunnel under a closed ward-gate
- a public hearing where neither side has full proof yet

Act One ends when the party proves that the same routing cipher moved both stolen ward-iron and living prisoners.

### Act Two - The Weight of Safe Roads

Fantasy dial: rising.

The investigation pushes to Survey Station Seven, a reclassified old road station four to five days beyond Stonewake's old network. Physical wardcraft and memory-erasure magic begin overlapping in visible ways.

Do not leap straight to cosmic revelations. Climb one rung at a time:

1. A ward-stone behaves wrong.
2. A route paper authenticates itself even after being burned.
3. A prisoner remembers a road that no map shows.
4. A repaired bridge refuses to carry one marked cart.
5. An altered road-warden speaks in two voices, one human and one infrastructure.
6. The old road layer responds to Brannok's brace and Selene's witness-seal.

Act Two ends with the heroes learning Thresh is not merely using the roads; he is trying to activate an old authority layer beneath them.

### Act Three - The Road That Remembers

Fantasy dial: high, earned.

The finale splits into two equal tracks:

- **The Public Track:** expose Thresh, prove the routing conspiracy, protect witnesses, prevent the government from burying the record.
- **The Underground Track:** stop the old survey engine from opening/closing roads according to Thresh's control system.

Brannok and Selene can choose which track they personally take, but both tracks matter. The Daughters, Mirelle, Voss, Kaelis, Zula, Jasna, Marcella, Kara, Eya, Needle, Mara, Pellan, Tovin, and others should be deployed by competence, not by spotlight theft.

The finale should require both kinds of heroism:

- Brannok refuses delay when delay would kill.
- Selene refuses erasure when erasure would make harm deniable.

## The ending

The best ending is not "everything is fixed."

The best ending is:

- the machine is broken;
- the names are on record;
- the roads are safer, not safe;
- the villains cannot hide behind procedure;
- the rescued people count;
- Brannok knows he did not wait;
- Selene knows she was remembered by choice, not capture;
- Rook tries to ride Ves or steal from Ves;
- Ves permits this only under conditions nobody else understands.
