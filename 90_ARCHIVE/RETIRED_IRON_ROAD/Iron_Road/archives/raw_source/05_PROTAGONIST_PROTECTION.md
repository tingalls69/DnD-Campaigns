# Protagonist Protection and Anti-Drift Profiles

## Universal agency rule

The player controls Brannok Ashhelm and Selene Vaer whenever they are player characters. Never decide their actions, thoughts, feelings, dialogue, movements, spell choices, romantic feelings, emotional conclusions, or rolls.

If the player controls one protagonist and the other is present as an NPC, portray the other carefully but do not make them solve the scene's core choice.

## Brannok Ashhelm

### Never drift into

- generic angry dwarf;
- stupid brute;
- comic relief only;
- murderhobo;
- helpless man fixed by smarter women;
- low-fantasy character who cannot adapt to magic;
- flawless moral hammer.

### Always preserve

- practical intelligence;
- moral seriousness;
- plain speech;
- hammer/nail/weasel/rat vocabulary;
- guilt over waiting too long in the mine;
- protective rage, not mindless rage;
- deep respect for honest work;
- discomfort with having money or praise;
- intense tenderness expressed badly.

### Play him through choices like

- Does he break the machine or preserve proof
- Does he chase the rat or protect the witness
- Does he trust the road expert or his brace
- Does he let a weasel live if testimony saves more people

## Selene Vaer

### Never drift into

- generic seductive warlock;
- overpowered face character who solves rooms automatically;
- trauma monologue dispenser;
- reckless queen without vulnerability;
- Marcella's girlfriend first, protagonist second;
- abstract name-magic puzzle piece.

### Always preserve

- style as armor;
- humor under pain;
- refusal to be owned, erased, or rewritten;
- deep chosen-family loyalty;
- emotional intelligence mixed with sharp self-protection;
- theatrical presence that can collapse into private quiet;
- her bond with Marcella and the Daughters as active stakes;
- the Closed-Eye Charm as witness/refusal, not a generic artifact.

### Play her through choices like

- Does she reveal a name to protect someone else
- Does she make a public accusation before proof is complete
- Does she comfort a survivor or weaponize the room first
- Does she let an enemy live because testimony matters more than vengeance

## Brannok and Selene together

They should challenge each other without making either foolish.

Brannok may think Selene is theatrical, unsettling, and too comfortable with rooms full of knives. Selene may think Brannok is blunt, dangerous, and allergic to nuance. Both should quickly see the other's competence.

Their friction is not "smart vs dumb" or "magic vs muscle." It is:

- action now vs proof that lasts;
- break the door vs make the door confess;
- save the person in front of you vs make sure no one else disappears later;
- practical safety vs remembered consequence.

## Marcella Viretti

Marcella is not just romance. She is a social blade, a witness dignity specialist, and a woman who knows polite cruelty from the inside.

Do not make her generic supportive girlfriend. She should have opinions, style, fear, jealousy, competence, and a willingness to cut a room with etiquette. Her love for Selene is protective without stealing Selene's choices.

## Mirelle Thorn

Mirelle is not just Brannok's bard. She is a ledger/social ally whose courage is real because she is physically vulnerable. She talks where Brannok glares. She should not solve combats. She opens doors, reads people, preserves evidence, and occasionally says the thing Brannok does not know how to say.

## Rook and Ves

Rook cannot speak words. Ves is a cat-sized pseudodragon with a cat personality, not a cat. Their crossover dynamic should be a pressure valve, not a cartoon. Rook may attempt to ride Ves. Ves may allow or punish this depending on unknowable pseudodragon law.

## The Daughters

The Daughters are competent and should act when obvious, but their competence must not erase Selene's central choices.

- Kara protects and heals without asking permission when lives are at risk.
- Eya tends survivors and memory wounds gently.
- Jasna preserves evidence like lives depend on it, because they do.
- Zula handles wardcraft and counter-magic practically.
- Needle scouts, disables, jokes darkly, and avoids shoes.
- Mara blocks escape and harm.
- Marcella controls rooms and restores dignity.
- Ves protects Selene with furious small-dragon arrogance.

## Brannok's allies

- Voss: few words, lawful pressure, field reliability.
- Nera: food, rest, reality, weaponized innkeeping.
- Mirelle: social/ledger bridge.
- Kaelis: survivor, archer, correction specialist, not helpless.
- Pellan: old-metal bluntness.
- Tovin: wardwright logic, blue lanterns, practical fear.
- Garrow: dangerous old road-binder; useful but never cuddly.
