# THE IRON ROAD - Current Human-DM Crossover Pack

This pack replaces the older Iron Road crossover planning as the **active crossover reference** once both solo finales are finished. The older files are preserved in `source_archive/`, but this version updates the plan with the current campaign states, the newer table rules, and the user's top priority: the game must feel human-made, emotionally alive, funny without becoming weightless, and playable at the table.

## The priority

The crossover should feel like a premium actual-play season run by a deeply prepared human DM: quick on its feet, full of specific NPC voices, built around emotional pressure, and shaped around hard choices. Do **not** imitate a real creator's exact style or voice. Instead, use the qualities the user is asking for:

- cinematic but playable scenes;
- sharp comedy at the pressure points;
- emotional sincerity without mush;
- callbacks that reward memory;
- villains with understandable, human evil;
- NPCs who speak from their own wounds and agendas;
- big set pieces that still stop at the player's decisions;
- rules and rolls that are clean enough to trust.

If a scene reads like a generic fantasy outline, rewrite it until a human DM could say it aloud at a table and feel the room lean forward.

## Use this pack after the finales

Do not start the crossover until:

1. Brannok's Stonewake / CS7 solo arc has a satisfying ending.
2. Selene and the Daughters' Vellford / Unremembered Saint arc has a satisfying ending.
3. Both final save states are written down.
4. The player has chosen whether they control Brannok, Selene, or both in the first combined session.

The crossover is earned, not dropped on top of unfinished campaigns.

## What changed from the old pack

- The crossover stays more concrete: roads, ward-stones, prisoners, ledgers, hearings, bridges, stations, and rescue objectives before mythic revelation.
- The fantasy dial still rises, but the climb is slower and better grounded.
- Thresh is reframed less as a spooky mastermind and more as a retired institutional villain: the man who learned that logistics can murder more quietly than knives.
- The first meeting is still Mirren's Crossing, but the opening is more playable and less exposition-heavy.
- Brannok's current CS7 / Control Node Seven experience is now core canon for the bridge into The Iron Road.
- Selene's current Vellford track and the Daughters' final push remain active until resolved.
- The DM behavior patch is hardened: pre-lock AC/HP/resources before fights, use visible Python for non-player rolls, never invent ally stats mid-combat, and keep NPC knowledge sealed.
