# Acts, Set Pieces, and Playable Beats

Each scene below has a concrete objective. Use them as modular beats, not rails.

## Act One: Meeting of Roads

### 1. Mirren's Crossing Bridge Failure

**Objective:** rescue a trapped survivor, stabilize a failing ward-bridge, secure the courier tube.

**Scene engine:** the bridge is actively cracking; a cleanup crew is due back; the tube is under a stone that cannot be moved without worsening the bridge.

**Complication:** Brannok wants the tube as road evidence. Selene wants it as witness-transfer evidence.

**Human-DM note:** Let the first friction be practical, not contrived. Two competent people reaching for the same proof at the same time is enough.

### 2. The Inn Where Nobody Signs Names

**Objective:** shelter witnesses and identify who warned the cleanup crew.

**Scene engine:** the innkeeper has learned that names on paper can get people killed, so every room is recorded as weather damage, broken shutters, or flour deliveries.

**Complication:** Marcella can dominate the room socially; Brannok can terrify liars; either approach may spook the innkeeper if overused.

### 3. The Road Works Archive Fire

**Objective:** save authentic records before a staged accident destroys them.

**Scene engine:** smoke in the rear archive, a locked evidence cage, a clerk pretending not to know where the key is, and a witness trapped in the copy room.

**Complication:** if the party saves the witness first, some records burn. If they save the records first, the witness may be hurt. Neither choice should be fake.

### 4. The First Public Accusation

**Objective:** force Road Works officials to acknowledge the shared cipher without revealing all evidence too early.

**Scene engine:** a local inquiry session. Thresh's people are not present in person, but their legal language is.

**Complication:** Selene is excellent in rooms; Brannok hates polished procedure. Give each a lever: Selene can pressure the room; Brannok can catch a practical lie about road conditions.

## Act Two: Weight of Safe Roads

### 5. The Map That Disagrees With Itself

**Objective:** follow a route that appears on cargo records but not on current maps.

**Scene engine:** the road is physically present at dawn, gone by noon, and back at dusk unless anchored by ward-iron.

**Complication:** this is one weird magical concept. Keep everything else concrete: wheel ruts, mile stones, cold iron, confused horses.

### 6. Survey Station Seven

**Objective:** infiltrate or assault the old station and rescue living prisoners held as route anchors.

**Scene engine:** station bells ring only when someone lies about where a prisoner is going.

**Complication:** Brannok's brace pulls toward the physical machinery; Selene's witness-seal pulls toward living captives. Both are true. They may need to split or prioritize.

### 7. The Altered Road-Warden

**Objective:** decide whether to save, contain, or destroy an altered person wired into the ward system.

**Scene engine:** the altered warden can keep a road open for refugees only by remaining connected to the station.

**Complication:** this is the moral wound scene. Brannok hates systems that turn people into supports. Selene hates systems that turn people into functions. Do not solve it with one spell unless the players earn that.

### 8. The Moving Prison Cart

**Objective:** stop a convoy moving prisoners through a warded detour before it crosses a line where pursuit becomes dangerous.

**Scene engine:** chase, road hazards, mounted guards, prisoners chained inside, false safe-route signs.

**Complication:** destroy the cart too hard and prisoners are hurt. Let tactics matter.

## Act Three: Road That Remembers

### 9. Capital Hearing / Underground Engine Split

**Objective:** expose Thresh publicly while stopping the old survey engine below the city.

**Scene engine:** two clocks run at once. Above, testimony can be buried by procedure. Below, ward-lines begin closing roads to isolate witnesses.

**Complication:** cut between tracks. No track is filler.

### 10. The Witness Roll Call

**Objective:** make the disappeared legally and magically count.

**Scene engine:** Jasna, Eya, Marcella, Mirelle, and rescued witnesses build the public record while enemies try to discredit, intimidate, or vanish witnesses.

**Complication:** Selene's presence can turn this from paperwork into consequence, but only if the witnesses choose to speak. Do not make them props.

### 11. The Hammer in the Engine

**Objective:** break or redirect the survey engine before it accepts Thresh's final closure order.

**Scene engine:** ward-stones, moving platforms, closing iron gates, old road authority that recognizes Brannok's brace but not necessarily Brannok's choices.

**Complication:** the engine offers Brannok the thing he wants: the ability to make roads safe by force. That is the temptation.

### 12. Final Choice: Safe Roads or Free Roads

**Objective:** choose what happens to the old authority layer.

Possible endings:

- **Break it:** roads become honest but harder to repair; no one can secretly control them again soon.
- **Bind it to public record:** safer roads, but requires ongoing oversight and risk of future abuse.
- **Give it witnesses:** the old system can only act when living witnesses and public records agree, a hard compromise requiring both protagonists' values.

The best ending depends on play. Do not predetermine it.
