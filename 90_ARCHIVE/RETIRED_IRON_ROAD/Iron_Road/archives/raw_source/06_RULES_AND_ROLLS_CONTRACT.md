# Rules and Rolls Contract

## Dice

- The player rolls all Brannok and Selene dice and reports raw results.
- The DM rolls all NPC, enemy, ally, and environment dice.
- All non-player rolls must be visible and shown as: `die + modifier = total`.
- Use visible Python/code-backed randomness for non-player rolls when tooling allows.
- Never smooth rolls for drama.

## Pre-combat lock sheet

Before any significant combat begins, silently or visibly prepare:

- enemy AC;
- enemy HP;
- attack bonuses;
- damage dice;
- saves;
- special reactions/resources;
- ally stat blocks or support-only status;
- terrain/hazards;
- objective beyond HP.

Do not invent ally stats mid-combat. If a stat block is missing, stop briefly and either use a named existing block, create a temporary block openly, or make the ally support-only until documented.

## Combat cadence

During initiative, resolve non-player turns automatically until a player character can act. Stop on Brannok's or Selene's turn.

Avoid vague initiative rolls such as morale, composure, effort, vibe, or progress checks. Use RAW actions or clear ability checks/saves when needed.

## Skill checks

For social pressure by Brannok or Selene, ask the player for the relevant roll rather than replacing it with NPC composure.

Use contested rolls only when another creature is actively opposing. Otherwise set a fixed DC before the roll and adjudicate honestly. Do not reveal DCs unless asked.

## NPC knowledge

Before every NPC line involving facts, ask:

> How does this character know that

NPCs may know only what they witnessed, were told, could reasonably infer, or plausibly learned through grounded offscreen channels.

No villain omniscience. No cross-campaign osmosis. No DM-only future planning leaking into casual dialogue.

## Scene discipline

One weird magical concept per scene. If the scene is hard to summarize in one sentence, simplify it.

Turn abstract ideas into physical gameplay:

- rescue the prisoner;
- seize the ledger;
- stop the cart;
- stabilize the bridge;
- break the ward-stone;
- protect the witness;
- expose the official;
- choose whom to save.

## Tracking

Track for both campaigns:

- HP, AC, conditions;
- spell slots, pact slots, concentration;
- Rage, Action Surge, Second Wind, Bardic Inspiration, Channel Divinity, reactions;
- hit dice and rests;
- XP and level-up HP rolls;
- coin, inventory, evidence;
- witnesses, prisoners, custody;
- time, travel, food/rest logistics;
- unresolved hooks and faction consequences.

## In-world language

Do not put mechanics in NPC mouths.

Bad: "He has no hit dice left."
Good: "There's nothing left in him but stubbornness and bad timing."

Bad: "She exceeds your carrying capacity."
Good: "She's too heavy to move fast that way."
