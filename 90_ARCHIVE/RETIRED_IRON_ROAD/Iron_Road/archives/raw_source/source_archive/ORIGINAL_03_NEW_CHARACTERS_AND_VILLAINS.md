## THE PRIMARY VILLAIN

### Orvaine Thresh - The Benefactor

**Retired Senior Commissioner, Continental Road Survey. Age 63.**

*Naming note: "Thresh" is deliberately distinct from Selene's "Thorne" and Brannok's "Thorn" (Mirelle). If it still reads too close at the table, rename her Orvaine **Vant** or Orvaine **Cole.***

**Who she is.** Gray, sharp-eyed, unhurried. Wears her pension badge without irony. Receives a modest stipend, keeps a small estate, is remembered by former colleagues as a *meticulous administrator*. Appears on no wanted list, no cult registry, no criminal record.

**What she actually is.** The architect of the entire forty-year operation. She built the routing system Carrow and Wynth exploited. She designed the accounting ciphers. She established the Road Survey's relationship with the Ashen Choir - *not* as a believer, but as their **logistics contractor.** She has never personally harmed anyone. She has authorized every piece of infrastructure that let thousands of people be disappeared.

**Her morality, exactly.** She is not evil the way a monster is evil. She is evil the way corrupt bureaucracy is evil: through abstraction, procedure, and the careful maintenance of distance between her decisions and their consequences. She built a system that *works*, and she will not allow it to be dismantled by a dwarf with a hammer and a warlock who collects witnesses.

**Her line, if ever cornered:** *"I made the roads safe. Everything else was the cost of the roads."*

**Combat.** She is not a fighter and should almost never be in melee. Her "stat block" is institutional: forty years of contacts, document control, political leverage, and contingency plans. Her physical defense is two Choir bodyguards (use a competent veteran/assassin profile) and a pre-planned escape route through Survey Headquarters. The real fight against Thresh is the **inquiry**, which is a social/evidence encounter, not initiative.

**Knowledge boundary.** Thresh knows the *whole* system - but she does not know what the party has recovered unless she's been told or could infer it from her informants. She does **not** know about the Original Survey's consciousness; she treats the ley-line chamber as a useful coincidence and an effective ritual space, nothing more. This blind spot matters: the ancient layer is something *she cannot perceive*, which is why the heroes can use it against her.

## THE SECONDARY VILLAIN

### The Ashen Choir Field Captain

**Carried over from Selene's finale. Give them the name you establish there.**

**Who they are.** A true believer in the Choir's "sorting the world is mercy" philosophy who has been seduced by practical power. They see the road network as righteous infrastructure for deciding who counts. Competent, dangerous, personally invested. They know Selene's name from the Reliquary arc and Brannok's face from a Stonewake notice by Act Two.

**Arc.** Escapes Selene's finale (earned, not cheap). Commands Survey Station Seven in Act Two - defeated there but **not killed.** Returns in Act Three as the commander of Thresh's capital kill-team. Their death or capture in Act Three should feel like the end of the *visible* enemy, just before Thresh reveals she was the invisible one all along.

**Combat.** A spellcasting-or-martial captain profile appropriate to your table's challenge level - think a cult fanatic leader with a couple of lieutenants and Choir transit magic. In Act Two, fold in the environmental ley-line surges. In Act Three, they fight in a city street under time pressure.

**Knowledge boundary.** Knows Choir doctrine and the operational details of transfers they personally ran. Knows Thresh exists as "the Benefactor" but may **not** know her real name or her full plan - Thresh compartmentalizes. They can give the party a direction, not the whole map.

## THE TERTIARY VILLAIN

### Massen Vir - The Accountant

**Male halfling. Keeper of the routing cipher's master keys.**

**Who he is.** Mild, fussy, forgettable - exactly the man you'd never suspect of holding the keys to a continental atrocity. Maintains the master cipher that translates routing marks into real destinations and real people.

**Function.** A slippery non-combatant who trades information for survival. **If captured early** (Act Two), he can significantly accelerate the investigation and hand the party a major advantage. **If he escapes**, he destroys the master keys on Thresh's order in Act Three Scene 9, which is what forces Brannok's underground race to protect the *physical* cipher keys.

He's a pressure valve the players control: catch him and the road gets shorter; miss him and the finale gets harder. Either outcome is a good story.

**Combat.** Negligible. Cunning Action, a smoke trick, a bolt-hole. He runs; he does not fight.

**Knowledge boundary.** Knows the cipher system cold. Knows Thresh by a code-name and a drop protocol. Does **not** know the Choir's theology or the ley-line layer - he's a pure logistics man who thinks he's laundering freight.

## THE MORAL COMPLICATION (not a villain)

### Tessa Carn - The Altered Road-Warden

**Former Continental Road Survey field warden. Disappeared three years ago.**

*Naming note: "Carn" is close to "Carrow." If it reads as confusing, rename her Tessa **Wend** or Tessa **Roe.***

**Who she was.** A field warden who personally certified the ward-iron components the Stonewake conspiracy later falsified. She noticed the falsification. She tried to report it. She was disappeared for it.

**What she is now.** The Choir's memory-alteration turned her into a living filing system: she recites correct route certifications in an endless loop, in a voice like prayer. Not dead. Not free. Found at Survey Station Seven.

**Her function in the story.** Brannok's first encounter with body-horror fantasy and his first "is this person beyond saving" gut-check that isn't Kaelis. Selene can *partially* restore her - never cleanly, this is not a tidy fix - and Brannok's road-warden vocabulary reaches her where magic alone stalls (she responds to specific maintenance terms). Restored even partly, she becomes the most valuable witness the party has, and her arc lands the season's thesis: *she did the right thing, she was destroyed for it, she survived, and she gets to say what happened.*

**Combat.** None by default. If the Choir tries to "reclaim" her, she's a protect-the-NPC objective, not a combatant.

**Knowledge boundary.** In her looped state she can only recite certifications - but the *content* of those recitations is real, recoverable evidence (correct ward-iron specs, real route data) if someone listens carefully. Partially restored, she remembers her own disappearance and who processed her, but only what she personally witnessed. She does not know Thresh's name unless she heard it directly.

## THE LONG-TERM COMPANION (built for Brannok's open slot)

Your Brannok notes specify the wanted companion precisely: a true adventuring-capable D&D character from the start, combat-competent, future-hero material, Charisma- or Intelligence-leaning, who joins for **moral/emotional reasons** rather than paperwork. Here is a character built to fit that slot exactly, introduced organically through action in Act One.

### Halla Brightwedge - The Surveyor Who Wouldn't Sign

**Proposed: Human (or half-elf) Paladin 1 / future Paladin, Oath of the Crown or Devotion - Charisma-leaning. Or, for an Intelligence lean, Artificer (Battle Smith).**

*Naming note: distinct from all existing names. If "Halla" reads too close to "Halven," rename her **Wren** Brightwedge.*

**Who she is.** A junior Continental Road Survey field surveyor, young, principled, and stubborn - the kind of person who reads the regulations *literally* and is horrified to discover everyone above her treats them as suggestions. She was assigned to certify the Mirren's Crossing ward-gate. She refused to sign off on components she knew were fake. For that refusal, she's been quietly reassigned, discredited, and is one bad day from being disappeared herself.

**How she enters (Act One, through action).** She's at Mirren's Crossing or the Map Room substation when the party arrives - investigating the same sabotage *from inside the bureaucracy*, terrified and alone, the only honest surveyor left on this stretch of road. When the Choir comes for her (or for the evidence she's hidden), she fights - clumsily but bravely - alongside the party. She joins not because anyone hands her a writ, but because these are the first people she's met who also refuse to sign the lie.

**Why she's the right companion for Brannok.** She is what Brannok wishes he'd been in the mine: someone who refused to wait, who said *no* to the people above her *before* anyone died. He sees his own failure redeemed in her courage, and she sees in him proof that refusing the lie doesn't have to end in being crushed. Their bond is mentor-and-mirror, and it's *moral and emotional*, exactly as specified. She also bridges both protagonists naturally - her Survey knowledge feeds Selene's investigation and her sword feeds Brannok's fights.

**Combat.** Start her low (Paladin 1 or Artificer 1) so she genuinely grows into a hero over the crossover - Charisma-based smites and Lay on Hands, or Battle Smith's steel defender and Intelligence-based attacks. She is *future* hero material, not a finished one. Let the player watch her level up alongside Brannok.

**Knowledge boundary.** She knows Survey procedure, the Mirren's Crossing certification she refused, and the names of the local officials who pressured her. She does **not** know the continental scope, the Choir, or Thresh - she's a low-level honest functionary who saw one corner of the rot. She learns the scale alongside the party, which keeps her from being an exposition fountain.

## SUPPORTING NPCS

### Doss Merren - The Frightened Innkeeper

Runs the coaching inn attached to the Map Room substation. Fifties, practical, scared. Has been passing courier documents she was told were "mercy welfare transports." Not a villain - a bystander folded into atrocity by a comfortable lie. Useful for a scene about *how ordinary people enable systems they don't understand.* Knows only the cover story and the faces of the couriers she's handled. *Naming note: "Merren" vs "Mirelle"/"Mirren's Crossing" - three near-collisions. Strongly consider renaming her **Doss Hale.***

### The Reformist Inquiry Chair - give them a name at the table

The head of the road-safety inquiry Thresh is scheduled to testify before. Genuinely reform-minded but procedurally bound - they can only act on admissible evidence, which is exactly the lever Thresh exploits. The Courtroom Track is partly about earning this person's trust and giving them what they need to act. Knows the politics of the Survey but not the criminal operation; they're being *played* by Thresh until the party proves otherwise.

## THE DOUBLE-VOSS PROBLEM (read before merging)

Both campaigns have a "Voss." Brannok's is **Sergeant Elian Voss** (male, Stonewake Ward). Selene's is **Sister-Captain Ameline Voss** (Reliquary). In the combined campaign this is genuinely confusing. **Resolve it before session one of the merge.** Cleanest fix: keep Elian Voss as "Voss," rename the Sister-Captain (e.g., Sister-Captain Ameline **Calder** - though note Brannok's Crowstep "Captain Calder Rusk," so pick a third clean surname like **Ameline Brask**). The combined prompt (`06`) flags this as a session-zero item.
