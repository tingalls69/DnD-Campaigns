# FINALE BRIDGES - How to plant the crossover in each solo finale

### So the merge feels inevitable, not bolted on

The crossover only works if each campaign reaches its own satisfying ending *first*, and each ending quietly leaves one thread hanging that the other campaign turns out to be holding. This file tells you exactly what to plant, and - just as importantly - what **not** to resolve.

The golden rule from your own crossover note: **do not merge campaigns before finales, and do not make either protagonist secondary.** These bridges respect that. Each finale stands completely on its own. The bridge is a single loose thread, not a cliffhanger.

-----

## BRANNOK'S FINALE - "THE STONEWAKE RECKONING"

### Where he is now

Fighter 5, downtime in Stonewake, heading to Pellan Orsik. The Carrow/Wynth conspiracy is in custody. He's earned a breath. His finale should let him *finish the Stonewake story* before anything continental opens up.

### The shape of his finale (one full arc, not a single scene)

Run the downtime beats you already have queued (Pellan, Bessa Vell, Kaelis/Tav follow-ups, the compensation claim). Then, within a session or two, surface a field mission from the rescued Road Works drafts - exactly as your active outline suggests. That field mission becomes the finale.

**The finale arc:** a damaged bridge or ward-gate where the "broken" components were *deliberately removed and replaced with fakes* - not for local profit, but to open a corridor. Brannok stabilizes the road, protects the crew or caravan, and confronts a remaining Carrow/Wynth associate. Classic, concrete, grounded. This is the satisfying capstone to his low-fantasy road-warden story.

### The ONE thread to plant (and not resolve)

While processing the seized Carrow ledger, **Mirelle** finds routing entries that don't fit the local corruption pattern. Some ward-iron logged as stolen or destroyed was actually *redirected* - routed somewhere, under a cipher that doesn't match anything from Stonewake. The destination mark reads:

> **▲ | CS7 | RW-ROUTING | IRON TRANSFER AUTHORIZED - O. THRESH, COMMISSIONER (RET.)**

The name **Orvaine Thresh** means nothing to anyone. A retired official, a pension, no record. The mark **CS7** is an old Continental Survey accounting code, obsolete for decades.

**Pellan Orsik is the key.** When Brannok brings the hammer to be looked over, let Pellan recognize the cipher *if Brannok shows it to him* - it's an old road-warden's accounting mark from a system retired fifty years ago. Pellan has seen the stamp once before, on a discredited batch of road components that nearly killed three road crews years ago. He doesn't make a speech. He hands the hammer back, clean and rebalanced, and says something like: *"Whatever's at the other end of those marks - take the hammer."*

### What this does

It tells Brannok, and the player, that the conspiracy he just dismantled was a *regional franchise of something continental* - and that the trail leads out of Stonewake entirely, toward a region he doesn't know. It reframes his whole campaign in one quiet beat: he's been fighting a symptom. That's the doorway to the crossover.

### What NOT to do in his finale

- Do **not** reveal the Choir, the erasure pipeline, or anything supernatural. Brannok's finale stays grounded. The weird stuff is the crossover's job.
- Do **not** introduce Selene or any Daughter. They arrive in Act One of the crossover, as strangers.
- Do **not** resolve the Thresh thread. It's a name and a cipher. Nothing more.

### Level target for crossover readiness

Aim for **Fighter 6** by the end of his finale (the next ASI/feat tier), so he steps into the crossover with a little more durability for genuinely supernatural threats. He still rolls his own level-up HP.

### The companion question

Your notes say Brannok wants a true long-term adventuring companion - combat-capable, future-hero material, Charisma- or Intelligence-leaning, joining for moral/emotional reasons. **Do not force one into the finale.** The crossover provides the perfect organic answer: see `03_NEW_CHARACTERS_AND_VILLAINS.md` for the companion built to fill exactly that slot, introduced through action in Act One.

## SELENE'S FINALE - "THE UNREMEMBERED SAINT"

### Where she is now

Warlock 9, in the lower mercy chapel of Saint Orveth's Reliquary with Thorne under Zone of Truth, prisoners to rescue, a hidden tunnel about to open. Your next-session outline already walks her into the tunnel crawl, the first rescue combat, and a safehouse lead. That sequence *is* the spine of her finale.

### The shape of her finale (one full arc)

Run the tunnel crawl and rescue exactly as outlined. Recover the safehouse lead. Then escalate to the safehouse raid, and from there to the **Unremembered Saint** itself - revealed not as a metaphor but as a *place*: an abandoned waystation reclassified off the maps using old Road Works paperwork, converted into a processing facility. The Daughters free the prisoners held there, destroy the ward-stones (which have been repurposed to *trap and muffle* people rather than protect roads), and stop the Ashen Choir field captain running the site. This is the dramatic, consequence-heavy capstone her campaign has been building toward.

### The ONE thread to plant (and not resolve)

Let the **field captain escape the final raid** with a wagon schedule and a sealed route document. The seal is the catch: it is **not Choir magic.** It's an official **Road Works Survey seal** - the kind issued by a government road-survey committee.

And: one of the rescued prisoners at the Unremembered Saint was transported from *far away*, through multiple stops. Their final routing document carries a freight mark that belongs to no church or cult - a **road-warden's commercial cipher**. The Daughters won't recognize it. But it means someone *outside* the Choir is running the physical logistics of the whole pipeline.

### What this does

It tells Selene, and the player, that the Ashen Choir is not the top of the pyramid - it's the *distribution arm* of something institutional and secular that predates it and will outlast it. The erasure she's been fighting rides on a road-safety bureaucracy she's never had reason to look at. That's her doorway to the crossover: the realization that to end the erasure for good, she has to follow paperwork into a world of trade roads, ward-iron, and a hammer-wielding stranger who's been pulling the same thread from the other side.

### What NOT to do in her finale

- Do **not** reveal Orvaine Thresh by name yet. The seal and the cipher are enough. The name belongs to Act Two of the crossover.
- Do **not** introduce Brannok or anyone from Stonewake. They meet as strangers at Mirren's Crossing.
- Do **not** resolve the field captain. They *must* survive to return in the crossover's Act Two as the commander of Survey Station Seven, with personal history against Selene intact.
- Keep the field captain's escape *earned*, not cheap - give them a real reason and a real cost, so it reads as a villain getting away rather than the DM protecting a plot device.

### Level target for crossover readiness

Aim for **Warlock 10** by the end of her finale (a new invocation and the weight of everything she's seen), so she enters the crossover at a tier comfortably matched to Brannok's Fighter 6 and her own Daughters.

-----

## THE SHARED OBJECT THAT LINKS BOTH FINALES

Both finales independently surface the **same routing cipher** - Brannok via the CS7 ledger mark, Selene via the prisoner's commercial freight mark. Neither party can read it fully. That shared, unreadable cipher is the literal object the crossover opens on: the sealed message tube on the dead courier at Mirren's Crossing carries the *complete* version of it, and only by combining Brannok's road-context and Selene's magical-forensic reading can it be decoded.

Plant the *partial* cipher in each finale. Let the *complete* one wait for the crossover. That's the hook that makes two finished stories click together like two halves of a broken seal.

-----

## TIMING AND TONE BETWEEN FINALES AND CROSSOVER

In-world, leave a gap. Both protagonists return to ordinary life for a while after their finales - Brannok to Stonewake and his locked chest at Carter's Rest, Selene to the Daughters and whatever passes for rest. The crossover opens when the loose thread *pulls them out* of that ordinary life. The crossover note's instruction holds: this is a **new post-finale arc, not a mid-campaign detour.** Give each ending room to breathe before the roads cross.
